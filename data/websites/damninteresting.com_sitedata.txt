Ulysses S. Grant led Union troops to victory in the American Civil War, then thwarted attempts by President Andrew Johnson to suppress fundamental civil rights of newly freed black Americans. Twice elected president himself, Grant stewarded a war-torn nation as it struggled to reunify. After leaving the White House, he invested his name and entire life savings to a Wall Street brokerage firm. It would make him rich, he was told, and afford him a comfortable retirement. Instead, it would leave him penniless. </p>\n<p>Like any army commander, Grant had lost battles and had known the pain of defeat. But this loss hit personally. Never before had he found himself in straits so dire, literally destitute. Fortunately, the former president and retired general had one more fight in him—because his real troubles had just begun. </p>\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<p class=\"nav-links\"><a href=\"https://www.damninteresting.com/the-reconstruction-of-ulysses-s-grant/#read-more\">Continue reading→</a></p>\n\t\t\t\t\t\t</div>\n\t\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\t</div>\n\n\t\t\t\n\t\t\n\t\t</div>\t\t\n\t</div>\n</div>\n\n<div class=\"page-row\">\n\t<div class=\"content\">\n\n\t\t<div class=\"width-6-col features\">\n\n\t\t\t<h3 class=\"column-header\">created</h3>\n\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<div class=\"nugget\">\n\t\t\t\t<header>\n\t\t\t\t\t<h3 class=\"section-label\">LATEST NUGGET:</h3>\n\t\t\t\t\t<h2 class=\"entry-title\"><a href=\"https://www.damninteresting.com/nugget/short-of-giants/\" title=\"Damn Interesting\" rel=\"bookmark\">Short of Giants</a></h2>\n\t\t\t\t</header>\t\t\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\t<figure class=\"align-right entry-image\" style=\"width:340px;\"><img src=\"https://damn-damninterestingc.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/03/potsdam.jpg\" alt=\"\" title=\"\" width=\"340\" height=\"348\" style=\"width:340px;max-height:348px;\" data-embiggen=\"true\"></figure>\n<p>In the 17th Century there was a shortage of giants in Europe, and only one man was to blame. The giant-greedy Frederick the First of Prussia.</p>\n<p>The king’s agents fanned out across Europe, on the lookout for tall men to press into the fabled Grand Grenadiers of Potsdam.  Diplomats trying to get on Frederick’s good side quickly learned to send Freddy larger-than-normal men as human presents.  Every year the Russian Tsar Peter the Great–who stood at six foot seven inches tall himself–made a gift of fifty giants. Once, when Peter took back an especially large specimen and replaced him with a shorter one, Frederick refused to speak to any Russian diplomat for months. “The wound,” the King explained, “is still too raw.” Fredrick even tried to ensure a race of giants by forcing all the tall men in Prussia to marry tall women.</p>\n<p>Though King Frederick wouldn’t ever dare risk his giants in anything resembling an actual war, he didn’t let his giant army just gather dust in a cupboard.
