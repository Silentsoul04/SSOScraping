colspan=\"2\">\n\t\t\t\t<p>\nBrief Introduction to \"Quran Tafsir\" What it is and Why We Need it\nWe start with the word \"Quran\"\n</p>\n<p>\nThe word \"Quran\" in Arabic means \"Recitation\" (not written word). Too many people today are telling us <b>what the Quran of Allah is and what it means.</b>\n</p>\n<p>\nAll too often we are hearing from both Muslims and Non-Muslims; 'The Quran says this-or-that and the Quran means so-and-so' without giving reference to their actual source for meaning and understanding.\n</p>\n<p>\nMany misconceptions, misquotes, misunderstandings and misrepresentations are tossed around in the media, news and public discussions about Islam and what it teaches.\n</p>\n<p>\nThe solution is to go back to the original source in the original language as understood by the people who first heard the Quran from the lips of the Prophet Muhammad, peace be upon him, along with his own definitions and explanations.\n</p>\n<p>\nThe good news is the Quran exists today in its entirety, exactly as it at the time of the prophet, peace be upon him.\n</p>\n<p>\nThe prophet Muhammad, peace be upon him, heard the words directly from the Angel Gabriel (Jibril in Arabic) and these words were memorized by Muhammad, peace be upon him, and then recited to his companions, who then memorized and passed on these same words to their followers and this has continued to this very day.\n</p>\n<p>\nThe first word Angel Gabriel spoke to Muhammad, peace be upon him, was \"Iqra\", which comes from the same root for Quran, and it was a command to <b>\"Recite!\"</b> (Many people, even Muslims today think this word meant 'read', but it is incorrect because the prophet Muhammad, peace be upon him, was not able to read or write). Immediately we see the importance of going back to the most authentic and original source - the Quran in Arabic - to better understand the meanings.\n</p>\n<b>The Word TAFSIR </b>is an arabic word <span class=\"AyahArabicW\" style=\"font-family: 'Traditional Arabic'\">تفسير  </span>which is derived from the root<i> 'fassara'  </i> . Someone who writes tafsir is a 'mufassir...\". IBN KATHIR - Famous Mufassir (commentator of Quran) <span class=\"AyahArabicW\" style=\"font-family: 'Traditional Arabic'\">مفسر   </span>. Well known and respected for his keen memory and a highly regarded scholar of tafsir (commentary) - Born 1302 AD. Memorized entire Quran, word-for-word along and memorized huge number of sayings and teachings (hadeeth) of the prophet Muhammad, peace be upon him, along with sayings and commentary about Quran from Muhammad's companions, may Allah be pleased with them.
