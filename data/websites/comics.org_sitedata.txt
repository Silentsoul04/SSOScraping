With a distinctive appearance because of its use of typography, the strip had numerous reprints and was adapted into a 1940s stage production. For a year, Morley collaborated on the writing of the strip with Ted Ferro.<br><br>In September 1947, Johnson began scripting again, with Morley doing the art. Johnson assisted Morley by giving him specific layouts for each panel, and the credit \"Jack Morley and CJ\" was then used on the strip. – 19 February 2010, USA) was a comics creator who is best known for drawing war stories at DC Comics in the 1950s and 1960s.<br><br>His career began in the late 1940s as an assistant to Will Eisner, primarily on the syndicated strip ‘The Spirit’. He also drew ‘Secret Files of Dr. Drew’ in “Rangers Comics” and ‘Señorita Rio’ in “Fight Comics”, both of which the Eisner Studio supplied to Fiction House.<br><br>From 1951, Grandenetti began working nearly two decades at DC. He drew stories for a lot of genre anthology series — “Western Comics”, “Mr. District Attorney”, “Strange Adventures”, “House of Mystery”.<br><br>His signature work was on war stories and covers. From 1952 to 1974, he drew some 260 covers for DC, almost all of them on the Big Five war titles.<br><br>He drew the ‘Gunner and Sarge’ feature in “All-American Men of War” and then “Our Fighting Forces” (1959–1965). Also in 1959, he and writer Robert Kanigher created ‘Mlle. Marie’ in “Star Spangled War Stories”.<br><br>In the late 1960s and early 1970s, he drew for Warren’s black-and-white magazines using a looser and more expressionist style than the stark realism that was demanded for the war stories.<br><br>At DC, he drew “The Spectre” (1966–1969), revived ‘The Phantom Stranger’ in “Showcase” (1969) with Mike Friedrich, and co-created ‘Nightmaster’ in “Showcase” (1969) with Dennis O’Neil.<br><br>He and his longtime friend Joe Simon created “Prez” (1973), provided all the stories in “Champion Sports” (1973–1974), and created ‘The Green Team’ in “1st Issue Special” (1975).<br><br>While he continued to publish occasional freelance comics work in the 1980s, he turned primarily to advertising. From 1990, he was an art director at Young &amp; Rubicon. He also painted, in a variety of media.<br><br>Grandenetti is one of the artists whose comics work was sampled by Pop Art painter Roy Lichtenstein in 1962 and 1964.<br><br>At Comiclopedia — <a href=\"https://www.lambiek.net/artists/g/grandenetti_jerry.htm\" rel=\"nofollow\">https://www.lambiek.net/artists/g/grandenetti_jerry.htm</a><br>At Wikipedia — <a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jerry_Grandenetti\" rel=\"nofollow\">https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jerry_Grandenetti</a><br>In the GCD — <a href=\"http://ow.ly/4mIn4T\" rel=\"nofollow\">http://ow.ly/4mIn4T</a><br><br>(Grandenetti created the cover of “All-American Men of War” #56, April 1958)\n<p></p>\n<img width=\"150px\" src=\"https://scontent.xx.fbcdn.net/v/t1.0-9/17952819_1299790326723533_1069220808686221801_n.jpg?oh=389da1f4b9e8adee1ae60eb936b88ddf&amp;oe=5996C460\">\n<a href=\"https://www.facebook.com/GrandComicsDatabase/photos/a.226127824089794.47121.136512123051365/1299790326723533/?type=3\"><img alt=\"comment on facebook\" src=\"/static/img/gcd/social/facebook_32.png\"></a>\n<a href=\"https://plus.google.com/+GrandComicsDatabase\"><img alt=\"google+\" src=\"/static/img/gcd/social/google_plus_32.png\"></a>\n<a href=\"mailto:?body=&amp;subject=\"><img alt=\"email\" src=\"/static/img/gcd/social/email_32.png\"></a>\n</div>\n<hr class=\"feed-post\">\n\n<div class=\"feed-post\">\nKatsuhiro Otomo (大友 克洋, born 14 April 1954, Japan) is a manga artist, screenwriter, and film director. He is best known as the creator of the manga “Akira” and its animated film adaptation.<br><br>His career began in 1973 and in 1979 he created his first longer work, ‘Fireball’, in “Action Deluxe”. His feature ‘Dōmu’ (“Young Magazine”, 1980–1982) received the 1983 Nihon SF Taisho Award, given by the Science Fiction and Fantasy Writers of Japan.<br><br>Otomo’s next feature in “Young” would become his most famous — ‘Akira’ (1982–1990). While the serial was still running, he created the animated adaptation “Akira” (1988). Both the books and the anime remain globally popular and highly regarded.<br><br>His other anime works include “Steamboy” (2004) and one of the stories in “Short Peace” (2013).<br><br>Otomo was decorated for his accomplishments by France in 2005 and 2014, and was awarded the Purple Medal of Honor by Japan in 2013.<br><br>He was inducted into the Will Eisner Award Hall of Fame (2012) and he has received the Winsor McCay Award for animation (2014) and the Grand Prix at Angoulême (2016).<br><br>At Comiclopedia — <a href=\"https://www.lambiek.net/artists/o/otomo_katsuhiro.htm\"
