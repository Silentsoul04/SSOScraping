A large, <a href=\"/info/Corvus_(genus)\" title=\"Corvus (genus)\">crow</a>-sized <a href=\"/info/Falcon\" title=\"Falcon\">falcon</a>, it has a blue-grey back, barred white underparts, and a black head and \"moustache\". As is typical of <a href=\"/info/Avivore\" title=\"Avivore\">bird-eating</a> raptors, peregrine falcons are <a href=\"/info/Sexual_dimorphism\" title=\"Sexual dimorphism\">sexually dimorphic</a>, females being considerably larger than males. The peregrine is renowned for its speed, reaching over 322&nbsp;km/h (200&nbsp;mph) during its characteristic hunting stoop (high speed dive), making it the <a href=\"/info/Fastest_animals\" title=\"Fastest animals\">fastest member of the animal kingdom</a>. According to a <a href=\"/info/National_Geographic_Channel\" title=\"National Geographic Channel\">National Geographic</a> TV programme, the highest measured speed of a peregrine falcon is 389&nbsp;km/h (242&nbsp;mph).</p>\nThe peregrine's breeding range includes land regions from the <a href=\"/info/Arctic_tundra\" title=\"Arctic tundra\" class=\"mw-redirect\">Arctic tundra</a> to the <a href=\"/info/Tropics\" title=\"Tropics\">tropics</a>. It can be found nearly everywhere on Earth, except extreme <a href=\"/info/Polar_region\" title=\"Polar region\" class=\"mw-redirect\">polar regions</a>, very high mountains, and most <a href=\"/info/Tropical_rainforest\" title=\"Tropical rainforest\">tropical rainforests</a>; the only major ice-free landmass from which it is entirely absent is <a href=\"/info/New_Zealand\" title=\"New Zealand\">New Zealand</a>. This makes it the world's most widespread raptor and one of the most widely found bird species.
