class=\"image\"><img src=\"/images/stories/ecole/2016-vue-tbi1-web.jpg\" alt=\"\" width=\"300em\"></span>Located in Brest, at the western tip of France in Brittany, ENIB is one of the highly selective French <em>Grandes Ecoles d’Ingénieurs</em>. It is a <strong>graduate school of engineering</strong> with a rigorous selection procedure and a <strong>high level teaching programme</strong>.&nbsp;The National Engineering School of Brest is accredited by the <em>Commission des Titres d’Ingénieur</em> (CTI), the independent organisation responsible for the accreditation of engineering curricula and higher education institutions in the field of engineering. As part of the Brest-Iroise technology cluster, which houses leading laboratories and innovative companies, ENIB enjoys an exceptional setting right by the sea.</p>\n<h3>Master’s Degree in Engineering</h3>\n<p>The 5-year engineering degree (Master’s Degree in Engineering) graduates engineers in the fields of <strong>Electronics</strong>, <strong>Computer Science</strong> and <strong>Mechatronics</strong>.&nbsp;The programme is entirely semester-based: <strong>each semester of study is run twice in the same academic year</strong> (a fall session starting in September and a spring session starting in February).&nbsp;</p>\n<h3>Masters of Science (Research)</h3>\n<p>The ENIB also offers <strong><a title=\"4 Masters of Science\" href=\"http://www.enib.fr/fr/formation/formation-superieure/masters-recherche\">4 Masters of Science</a></strong> (research-oriented):</p>\n<ul>\n<li>Master in Photonics</li>\n<li>Master in Signal Processing</li>\n<li>Master in Computer Science</li>\n<li>Master in Materials and Structures</li>\n</ul>\n<blockquote class=\"blockquote-terracotta-gray\">\n<h3>Download documents Information for title=\"Course programme\" href=\"http://www.enib.fr/files/plaquettes/2015-2016-livret-programme-cours-enib-eng.pdf\" target=\"_blank\"><strong>Course programme</strong>&nbsp;</a></li>\n<li>Academic foreign academic institutions. These dynamics is reflected in publications (journals, books), but also conferences, patents... All of these activities and outputs contribute to the international reputation of research at ENIB.</p>\n<h3>2 research laboratories at the forefront of innovation:</h3>\n<ul>\n<li><strong><a title=\"IRDL\" href=\"http://irdl.fr/\" target=\"_blank\">IRDL</a></strong> (in the field of mechanical systems and materials engineering)</li>\n<li><strong><a title=\"Lab-Sticc\" href=\"http://www.lab-sticc.fr/en/index/\" target=\"_blank\">Lab-Sticc</a></strong> (in the field of Information and communication science and technology)</li>\n</ul>\n<h3>A research center: the European Research Center for Virtual Reality (<a title=\"CERV\" href=\"http://www.cerv.fr/?page_id=39&amp;lang=en\" target=\"_blank\">CERV</a>)</h3>\n<p>The CERV’s research focuses on virtual reality, autonomous behaviours, intelligent environments, Human-Machine interaction, and the modeling and simulation of complex systems.</p>\n<h3>Research Focus and Areas of Expertise</h3>\n<p>ENIB has developed strong expertise in 4 main scientific areas closely linked to the courses taught during the programme:</p>\n<ul>\n<li>Virtual reality for education, leisure and culture</li>\n<li>Engineering and computing sciences for biology and health</li>\n<li>Materials and adaptive systems for marine environments</li>\n<li>Photonic, optoelectronic and digital devices for very high-speed communications and for marine applications&nbsp;</li>\n</ul>\n<h2>About engineering education in France</h2>\n<p><span class=\"image\"><img src=\"/images/stories/international/engineering-studies-woman.jpg\" alt=\"\" width=\"230em\"></span>France is famous for the quality of its engineering programmes which combine rigorous training in science with practical training in engineering. The close relations between French engineering schools and industry and business enhance the quality and relevance of the education students receive.</p>\n<p>ENIB is one of the highly selective French <em>Grandes Ecoles d’Ingénieurs</em>. In France, there are about 240 such engineering schools. These small to middle-sized institutions share common features including their small workshop sessions, high teaching standards, innovative pedagogical methods and selective recruitment process. Their curriculum comprises high level training in project management, economics and human sciences. They are strongly vocationally oriented and organised in cooperation with industry partners. They also provide work experience placements.</p>\n<p>The engineering degree called in France <em>Diplôme d’ingénieur</em> is a highly esteemed degree. It can only be delivered by a French engineering school accredited by the French National Commission for Engineering Degrees (CTI). CTI (<em>Commission des Titres d'Ingénieur</em>) is an independent body established by French law. Its legal missions are: the evaluation and assessment of higher education institutions in the fields of engineering, computer science, applied mathematics, project management; the development of quality in engineering education; the promotion of engineering curricula and careers in France and abroad.</p>
