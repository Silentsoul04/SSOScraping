AyaBambi Distort Reality In This Unreal Face-Hacking Video\">\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t</a>\n\t\t\t\t\t</div><!--/.post-thumbnail-->\n\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\t<div class=\"entry excerpt\">\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t<p>“Nice face. I’d like to wear it sometime.” No, it’s not a Valentine’s Day card from Buffalo Bill. But it might be an icebreaker in 2020.</p>\n<p><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">If you’ve had a bad face day, you can usually keep up appearances on social media thanks to the magical filters of Snapchat and Instagram. If you’re having a really bad case of the fugs, you can just face-swap yourself with your favorite movie star and call it quits. That’s all well and good from the privacy and solitude of your own bed, but when it comes to heading out into the real world, the closest thing to a real-life filter </span><i><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">was</span></i><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\"> a good concealer.</span></p>\n<p><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">But now, thanks to an innovative dream team from Tokyo, IRL Snapchat filters are real. Sort of.</span></p>\n<h2><b>From Haunted Rides To Kat Von D</b></h2>\n<div style=\"width: 510px\" class=\"wp-caption alignnone\"><img class=\"size-medium\" src=\"http://promapcentral.wpengine.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/tumblr_lts4torIKW1r337tvo1_500.jpg\" alt=\"Projection mapping at Disneyland, 1969\" width=\"500\" height=\"167\"><p class=\"wp-caption-text\">Projection mapping at Disneyland, 1969 | <a href=\"http://projection-mapping.org/the-history-of-projection-mapping/\">Source</a></p></div>\n<p><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">The practice has moonlighted under a few aliases, including “spatial augmented reality” and gruesome-sounding “face-hacking.” But it’s commonly known as projection mapping. In short, the practice involves projecting an image onto a non-flat surface. The </span><a href=\"http://projection-mapping.org/the-history-of-projection-mapping/\"><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">earliest adopter of projection mapping</span></a><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\"> was none other than Disney, which first used projection mapping at the Haunted Mansion ride at Disneyland way back in 1969. It’s been used on-and-off since then with limited success. But the capabilities of projection mapping have always been pretty basic … up until now.</span></p>\n<p><a href=\"http://www.w0w.co.jp/about/\"><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">WOW labs</span></a><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">, helmed by CGI-expert Nobumichi Asai, has been working on an advanced method of projection mapping for the last few years. </span></p>\n<p><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">So, what is it?</span></p>\n<p><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">Asai’s projection mapping uses specialized software to track a surface in motion, while a custom-built projector alters the image to adjust to the subject’s movements. The results are a seamless and high-definition projection that works in tandem with the face. For instance, say you wanted to project a zombie mask onto a model. If the model moved, the mask would move with them — without distorting or losing quality. It gives the appearance of a CGI effect in real life. </span></p>\n<p><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">In 2015, Kat Von D used projection mapping software to promote her new collection of makeup at Sephora. The projection mapping used was 240 frames per second (fps).</span></p>\n<div style=\"width: 550px\" class=\"wp-caption alignnone\"><img src=\"http://wildbytes.cc/wp-content/uploads/2015/12/KVD_Sephora_Wildbytes_03.gif\" alt=\"Kat Von D projection mapping\" width=\"540\" height=\"304\"><p class=\"wp-caption-text\"><a href=\"http://wildbytes.cc/work/kat-von-d-live-face-projection-mapping\">Source</a></p></div>\n<p><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">The latest production from Asai uses a projector that uses 1,000 fps — the fastest in the world.
