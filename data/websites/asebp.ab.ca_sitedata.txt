The ASEBP Trustees have recently completed their annual review of dental fees and increases within Alberta and across Canada. As previously reported, and recently confirmed by the provincial government, fees in Alberta are both unregulated and the highest in Canada. As we await the release of a public dental fee guide (recommendations for fees charged for dental services) by the Government of Alberta, ASEBP Trustees have chosen to continue to hold dental fee reimbursement rates at their 2015 levels for the 2017 benefit plan year (reimbursement rates were also held in 2016 to the 2015 levels). This means that if your dentist raises their rates in 2017, you may see an increase in out-of-pocket expenses.</div><div><br></div><div>We will continue to review (and adjust where necessary) the services offered through our Dental Care plans in 2017 to ensure they meet your needs and maintain the plan’s financial stability, including looking to address services that have little to no oral health benefit.
