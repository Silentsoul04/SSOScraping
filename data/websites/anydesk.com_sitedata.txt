For the development of AnyDesk, we took a step back and asked ourselves fundamental questions. How should remote desktop work? What is feasible with modern computer hardware? AnyDesk was designed from the ground up to be ground breaking. At its core is <a href=\"http://anydesk.com/technology\" class=\" Hyphenator962hide\">DeskRT</a>, a new video codec that is specifically designed for graphical user interfaces. That's why AnyDesk is one leap ahead.</p>\n<p class=\"hyphenate Hyphenator962hide\">AnyDesk is the fastest remote desktop software on the market. This is much more than any competing remote desktop application and makes on-screen movement as smooth as possible, as most PC displays also operate at 60 frames per second. AnyDesk makes jerk-free working possible.</p>  \n  \n  \n\n</div>\n\n</div>\n<div class=\"col-md-4\">\n<div class=\"ce_image block\">\n\n  \n  <figure class=\"image_container\">\n\n    \n    \n\n<img src=\"files/anydesk/img/contents/benchmark-latency.png\" width=\"300\" height=\"220\" alt=\"\" title=\"(less is better)\">\n\n\n\n    \n          <figcaption class=\"caption\">(less is better)</figcaption>\n    \n  </figure>\n\n\n</div>\n\n<div class=\"ce_text block\">\n\n  \n  \n              <h3>Latency</h3>\n          <p>Latency at the lowest possible level is critical for working on a remote desktop. The lower the latency, the quicker the remote PC responds to your input. Since internet connections always impose a certain latency when transmitting data from one PC to the other, it is very important that AnyDesk adds as little as possible to the bill. In local networks, AnyDesks latency lies below 16 milliseconds and is no longer perceptible.</p>  \n  \n  \n\n</div>\n\n</div>\n<div class=\"col-md-4\">\n<div class=\"ce_image block\">\n\n  \n  <figure class=\"image_container\">\n\n    \n    \n\n<img src=\"files/anydesk/img/contents/benchmark-bandwidth.png\" width=\"300\" height=\"220\" alt=\"\">\n\n\n\n    \n          <figcaption class=\"caption\">(less is better)</figcaption>\n    \n  </figure>\n\n\n</div>\n\n<div class=\"ce_text block\">\n\n  \n  \n              <h3>Traffic</h3>\n          <div class=\"grid-4 grid-m-12\">\n<div class=\"rightcol3\">\n<p class=\"hyphenate Hyphenator962hide\">If remote desktop software is used over an internet connection, the highest possible bandwith efficiency is the deciding factor.
