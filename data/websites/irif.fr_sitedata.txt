    <div class=\"dw-content \">\n<h3 class=\"sectionedit1\" id=\"institut_de_recherche_en_informatique_fondamentale\">Institut de Recherche en Informatique Fondamentale<a class=\"anchorjs-link \" anchorjs-icons; font-style: normal; font-variant-ligatures: normal; font-variant-caps: normal; font-weight: normal; line-height: 1; padding-left: 0.375em;\"></a></h3>\n<div class=\"level3\">\n\n<p>\n</p><div class=\"bs-wrap bs-wrap-well well \">\n<a href=\"https://www.irif.fr\" class=\"media\" title=\"https://www.irif.fr\" rel=\"nofollow\"><img src=\"https://www.irif.fr/_media/irif.svg?w=125&amp;tok=c570cc\" class=\"medialeft img-responsive\" title=\"IRIF\" alt=\"IRIF\" width=\"125\"></a>\n<a href=\"http://www.univ-paris-diderot.fr\" class=\"media\" title=\"http://www.univ-paris-diderot.fr\" rel=\"nofollow\"><img src=\"https://www.irif.fr/_media/logop7.svg?w=38&amp;tok=adc156\" class=\"mediaright img-responsive\" title=\"Université Paris 7\" alt=\"Université Paris 7\" width=\"38\"></a>\n<a href=\"http://www.cnrs.fr\" class=\"media\" title=\"http://www.cnrs.fr\" rel=\"nofollow\"><img src=\"https://www.irif.fr/_media/logocnrs.svg?w=90&amp;tok=ca36dc\" class=\"mediaright img-responsive\" title=\"CNRS\" alt=\"CNRS\" width=\"90\"></a>\nL'IRIF est une unité mixe de recherche (UMR 8243) du <a href=\"http://www.cnrs.fr\" class=\"urlextern\" title=\"http://www.cnrs.fr\" rel=\"nofollow\">CNRS</a> et de l'<a href=\"http://www.univ-paris-diderot.fr/\" class=\"urlextern\" \n</p></div>\n<p></p>\n\n<p>\n<br>\n\n</p>\n\n</div>\n\n<h4 id=\"poste_de_maitre_de_conferences\">Poste de maître de conférences<a class=\"anchorjs-link \" href=\"#poste_de_maitre_de_conferences\" aria-label=\"Anchor link for: poste_de_maitre_de_conferences\" data-anchorjs-icon=\"\" style=\"font-family: anchorjs-icons; font-style: normal; font-variant-ligatures: normal; font-variant-caps: sur les thématiques de l'unité. Consulter la <a href=\"https://www.irif.fr/_media/27mcf4305_informatique.pdf\" class=\"media mediafile mf_pdf\" title=\"27mcf4305_informatique.pdf (73.2&nbsp;KB)\">fiche de poste</a>. \n<br>\n\n</p>\n\n<p>\n<br>\n\n</p>\n\n</div>\n\n<h4 id=\"prochains_seminaires\">Prochains séminaires<a class=\"anchorjs-link \" href=\"#prochains_seminaires\" aria-label=\"Anchor link for: prochains_seminaires\" data-anchorjs-icon=\"\" style=\"font-family: anchorjs-icons; font-style: normal; font-variant-ligatures: normal; font-variant-caps: normal; font-weight: normal; line-height: 1; padding-left: 0.375em;\"></a></h4>\n<div class=\"level4\">\n<hr>\n\n</div>\n<div class=\"plugin_include_content plugin_include__db:seminaires\" id=\"plugin_include__db__seminaires\">\n<div class=\"level4\">\n\n\n\n<p>\n<span class=\"bs-wrap bs-wrap-label label label-primary\">Mardi 25 avril 2017 · 11h00 · Salle 1007</span>\n</p>\n\n<p>\n<a href=\"https://www.irif.fr/seminaires/algo/index\" class=\"wikilink1\" title=\"seminaires:algo:index\">Algorithmes et complexité</a> · Nicolas Flammarion (DI/ENS) · Optimal rates for Least-Squares Regression through stochastic gradient descent.<span class=\"bs-wrap bs-wrap-button\" data-btn-type=\"link\" data-btn-collapse=\"213\"><a href=\"javascript:void(0)\" data-toggle=\"collapse\" data-target=\"#213\" class=\"btn btn-link\" role=\"button\"><i class=\"fa fa-plus-circle\" style=\"\" title=\"\"></i></a></span>\n</p>\n<div class=\"bs-wrap bs-wrap-collapse collapse \" id=\"213\"><div class=\"bs-wrap bs-wrap-well well well-sm\">At a broad level, stochastic optimization is concerned with optimizing a function in the presence of noise. In this context, we consider the optimization of a quadratic objective function whose gradients are only accessible through a stochastic oracle that returns the gradient at any given point plus a zero-mean finite variance random error. We present the first algorithm that achieves the optimal prediction error rates for least-squares regression, both in terms of forgetting of initial conditions in O(1/n^2), and in terms of dependence on the noise and dimension d of the problem, as O(d/n). Our new algorithm is based on averaged accelerated regularized gradient descent, and may also be analyzed through finer assumptions on initial conditions and the Hessian matrix, leading to dimension-free quantities that may still be small while the “optimal” terms above are large.</div>\n</div><hr>\n\n<p>\n<span class=\"bs-wrap bs-wrap-label label label-primary\">Mardi 25 avril 2017 · 14h00 · Salle 1007</span>\n</p>\n\n<p>\n<a href=\"https://www.irif.fr/seminaires/adg/index\" class=\"wikilink1\" title=\"seminaires:adg:index\">Algorithmique distribuée et graphes</a> · Mike Molloy (University of Toronto and Ecole Normale Superieure Paris) · Entropy Compression and the Lovasz Local Lemma<span class=\"bs-wrap bs-wrap-button\" data-btn-type=\"link\" data-btn-collapse=\"241\"><a href=\"javascript:void(0)\" data-toggle=\"collapse\" data-target=\"#241\" class=\"btn btn-link\" role=\"button\"><i class=\"fa fa-plus-circle\" style=\"\" title=\"\"></i></a></span>\n</p>\n<div class=\"bs-wrap bs-wrap-collapse collapse \" id=\"241\"><div class=\"bs-wrap bs-wrap-well well well-sm\">The Lovasz Local Lemma, a cornerstone of the probabilistic method, is a powerful and widely used proof technique. In 2009, Moser introduced a technique called entropy compression to provide efficient algorithms which construct objects that the Local Lemma guarantees to exist. Recently, entropy compression has been used to develop more powerful versions of the Local Lemma which provide existence proofs in settings where the original Local Lemma does not apply.
