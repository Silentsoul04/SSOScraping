As usual it is held together with the&nbsp;<a href=\"http://www.pwg.org/\" class=\"urlextern\" title=\"http://www.pwg.org/\" rel=\"nofollow\">PWG</a>&nbsp;(Printing Working Group) Meeting, this time in Sunnyvale, California. We invite again printer manufacturers, developers of Linux printing components as CUPS, Ghostscript, Color Management, desktops, applications, of Linux distributions, … to plan and discuss on making printing under Linux “just work”. This time the sessions are again integrated with the sessions of the PWG, an <abbr title=\"Operating System\">OS</abbr>-independent standardization organization for digital printing.\n</p>\n\n</div>\n\n<h2 class=\"sectionedit3\" id=\"openprinting-summit-2014-together-with-pwg-meeting-in-toronto-canada-on-august-12-15\">OpenPrinting Summit 2014 together with PWG Meeting in Toronto, Canada on August 12-15</h2>\n<div class=\"level2\">\n\n<p>\nOur annual meeting, the <a href=\"/openprinting/openprinting-summitpwg-meeting-toronto-2014\" class=\"wikilink1\" title=\"openprinting:openprinting-summitpwg-meeting-toronto-2014\">OpenPrinting Summit</a> is approaching! This time it is held together with the&nbsp;<a href=\"http://www.pwg.org/\" class=\"urlextern\" title=\"http://www.pwg.org/\" rel=\"nofollow\">PWG</a>&nbsp;(Printing Working Group) Meeting in Toronto, Canada. We invite again printer manufacturers, developers of Linux printing components as CUPS, Ghostscript, Color Management, desktops, applications, of Linux distributions, … to plan and discuss on making printing under Linux “just work”. This time the sessions are again integrated with the sessions of the PWG, an <abbr title=\"Operating System\">OS</abbr>-independent standardization organization for digital printing.\n</p>\n\n</div>\n\n<h2 class=\"sectionedit4\" id=\"openprinting-is-participating-in-google-summer-of-code-2014\">OpenPrinting is participating in Google Summer of Code 2014</h2>\n<div class=\"level2\">\n\n<p>\nThis year two students are working on OpenPrinting projects:\n</p>\n<ul>\n<li class=\"level1\"><div class=\"li\"> Daniel Dressler: IPP Everywhere for USB printers: Add IPP-over-USB support to CUPS/Linux</div>\n</li>\n<li class=\"level1\"><div class=\"li\"> Joseph Simon: Complete IPP Everwhere support of CUPS: rastertopdf filter to accept PWG Raster as input format</div>\n</li>\n</ul>\n\n<p>\n&nbsp;\n</p>\n\n</div>\n\n<h2 class=\"sectionedit5\" id=\"pdf-is-the-standard-print-job-format-from-cups-16x-on\">PDF is the standard print job format from CUPS 1.6.x on</h2>\n<div class=\"level2\">\n\n<p>\nAll important desktop applications (GTK/GNOME, Qt/KDE, LibreOffice/OpenOffice.org, Firefox, Thunderbird, …) send print jobs in PDF and not in PostScript any more by default. In addition, a complete CUPS filter chain to process print jobs in PDF is available and used.\n</p>\n\n<p>\nCUPS author Mike Sweet/Apple have decided to not include the Linux-specific CUPS filters in the upstream CUPS source any more and we have agreed to <a href=\"/openprinting/cups-filters\" class=\"wikilink1\" title=\"openprinting:cups-filters\">maintain them at OpenPrinting</a>. Here we have done some clean-up and have discontinued the filters for the PostScript-centric workflow in favor of the PDF workflow, meaning that the upstream standard for CUPS under Linux (using CUPS plus our <a href=\"/openprinting/cups-filters\" class=\"wikilink1\" title=\"openprinting:cups-filters\">cups-filters</a> package) is the PDF-based job processing, letting every non-PDF input be converted to PDF first, page management options being applied by a pdftopdf filter and Ghostscript being called with PDF as input.\n</p>\n\n<p>\nOnly exception is if the input data is PostScript and the printer is a PostScript printer or the printer driver requires PostScript as input. The we do not turn PostScript into PDF and back into PostScript but pass the PostScript through the pstops filter as before.\n</p>\n\n<p>\nHaving this workflow we ask all driver developers kindly to not create any PPDs/drivers for non-Postscript printers which require exclusively PostScript. PPD files should at least accept PDF or CUPS Raster now. See also our <a href=\"/openprinting/writingandpackagingprinterdrivers\" class=\"wikilink1\" title=\"openprinting:writingandpackagingprinterdrivers\">driver design/packaging page</a>.\n</p>\n\n<p>\nMore info on our <a href=\"/openprinting/pdf_as_standard_print_job_format\" class=\"wikilink1\" title=\"openprinting:pdf_as_standard_print_job_format\">page about the PDF printing workflow</a>&nbsp;and on the <a href=\"/openprinting/cups-filters\" class=\"wikilink1\" title=\"openprinting:cups-filters\">cups-filters page</a>.\n</p>\n\n</div>\n\n<h2 class=\"sectionedit6\" id=\"making-printing-just-work-volunteers-andor-sponsors-needed\">Making Printing \"Just Work\" - Volunteers and/or Sponsors needed!</h2>\n<div class=\"level2\">\n\n<p>\nFor getting a great user experience with printing there is still a lot of coding needed. Your contribution, either work or funding, is highly appreciated. As we want our work to get a standard, we will let every completed project get into the major Linux distributions, so your work will help a lot of Linux users and will make Linux a better <abbr title=\"Operating System\">OS</abbr>.\n</p>\n\n<p>\nEnter the amazing world of free software and help fixing <a href=\"https://bugs.launchpad.net/bugs/1\" class=\"urlextern\" title=\"https://bugs.launchpad.net/bugs/1\" rel=\"nofollow\">bug #1</a> of Linux.\n</p>\n\n<p>\nCurrently, we appreciate volunteers/sponsors for these projects:\n</p>\n\n<p>\n<strong>Vendor WIN32/Mac <abbr title=\"Operating System\">OS</abbr> X drivers made available to Linux applications:</strong>\n</p>\n\n<p>\nMake many more printers working under Linux by creating a wrapper framework for the manufacturer's Windows/Mac <abbr title=\"Operating System\">OS</abbr> X drivers, like the ndiswrapper for WLAN cards.\n</p>\n\n<p>\n<strong>JTAPI implementation:</strong> The OpenPrinting workgroup has designed a Job Ticket <abbr title=\"Application Programming Interface\">API</abbr> (JTAPI) already. You can help us by writing an implementation of this <abbr title=\"Application Programming Interface\">API</abbr> (libjtapi). We especially also need an implementation of the Printer Working Group: Print Job Ticket (PWG:PJT).\n</p>\n\n<p>\nFind more information and contact info on our <a href=\"/openprinting/implementation\" class=\"wikilink1\" title=\"openprinting:implementation\">project implementation</a> page.\n</p>\n\n<p>\nWe appreciate your participation on these projects.\n</p>\n\n</div>\n\n<h2 class=\"sectionedit7\" id=\"for-developers\">For Developers</h2>\n<div class=\"level2\">\n\n<p>\nThe goal of the <a href=\"/openprinting/development\" class=\"wikilink1\" title=\"openprinting:development\">OpenPrinting workgroup</a> is to develop and promote a set of standards that will address the complete printing needs of embedded, mobile, desktop, enterprise, and production environments, including management, reliability, security, scalability, printer feature access and network accessibility.
