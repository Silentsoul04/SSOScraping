class=\"help_section\">The documents are returned sorted on relevance depending on order, proximity, frequency of terms.</div><h3>Advanced Search or Boolean Search</h3><div class=\"help_section\"><h4>Default search behavior</h4><p>By default, all search terms are <em>optional</em>.&nbsp;It behaves like an OR logic.&nbsp;Objects that contain the more terms are rated higher in the results and will appear first in their type. For example, <strong>wiki forum</strong> will find:</p><ul><li>objects that include both terms</li><li>objects that include the term <strong>wiki</strong></li><li>objects that include the term <strong>forum</strong></li></ul><h4>Requiring terms</h4><p>Add a plus sign ( + ) before a term to indicate that the term <em>must</em> appear in results.&nbsp;Example: <strong>+wiki forum</strong> will find objects containing at least <strong>wiki</strong>. Objects with both terms and many occurences of the terms will appear first.</p><h4 id=\"excluding\">Excluding terms</h4><p>Add a minus sign ( - ) before a term to indicate that the term <em>must not</em> appear in the results.&nbsp;To reduce a term's value without completely excluding it, <a href=\"#reducing\" title=\"Reducing a term's value\">use a tilde</a>.&nbsp;Example: <strong>-wiki forum</strong> will find objects that do not contain <strong>wiki</strong> but contain <strong>forum</strong></p><h4>Grouping terms</h4><p>Use parenthesis ( ) to group terms into subexpressions.&nbsp;Example: <strong>+wiki +(forum blog)</strong> will find objects that contain <strong>wiki</strong> and <strong>forum</strong> or that contain <strong>wiki</strong> and <strong>blog</strong> in any order.</p><h4>Finding phrases</h4><p>Use double quotes ( \" \" ) around a phrase to find terms in the exact order, exactly as typed.&nbsp;Example: <strong>\"Alex Bell\"</strong> will not find <strong>Bell Alex</strong> or <strong>Alex G. Bell</strong>. </p><h4>Using wildcards</h4><p>Add an asterisk ( * ) after a term to find objects that include the root word. For example, <strong>run*</strong> will find:</p><ul><li>objects that include the term <strong>run</strong></li><li>objects that include the term <strong>runner</strong></li><li>objects that include the term <strong>running</strong></li></ul><h4 id=\"reducing\">Reducing a term's value</h4><p>Add a tilde ( ~ ) before a term to reduce its value indicate to the ranking of the results.&nbsp;Objects that contain the term will appear lower than other objects (unlike the <a href=\"#excluding\" title=\"Excluding terms\">minus sign</a>\twhich will completely exclude a term).&nbsp;Example: <strong>+wiki ~forum</strong> will rate an object with only <strong>wiki</strong> higher that an object with <strong>wiki</strong> and  <strong>forum</strong>.</p><h4>Changing relevance value</h4><p>Add a less than ( &lt; ) or greater than ( &gt; ) sign before a term to change the term's contribution to the overall relevance value assigned to a object.&nbsp;Example: <strong>+wiki +(&gt;forum &lt; blog)</strong> will find objects that contain <strong>wiki</strong> and <strong>forum</strong> or <strong>wiki</strong> and <strong>blog</strong> in any order. <strong>wiki forum</strong> will be rated
