Obviously, this change has caused some growing pains. I’d like to take some time to address what necessitated these changes and what we are planning to do to address your concerns. \n\n\nHistorically, mid-level and low-level players have been able to comfortably keep up with our releases, as well as with other high-level players. This means that all players could compete at the highest level, earning all the latest content, and engage in our features without any sense of urgency. While this may have evened the playing field, it also meant that player progression ceased after a certain point. You could shoot up to the highest power level with no real structure, get all the tech you needed, and never have any motivation to engage with anything new.\n\n\nFor example, Dread Battleships were considered the ultimate asset for a fleet for several years. In that case, progression in the game stalled across the board and there existed no end-game content for VEGA Conflict players to engage with. To address this, we needed to refocus on end-game content to keep the game fresh and engaging, extending its longevity all around.\n\n\nThe first step in our process to do this was what you call “the Grand Rebalance”. This rebalance allowed us to adjust the fundamental building blocks of the game to a more normalized power curve. This, in turn, gave us more control over the targets we could create and the outcomes we expected when players went up against those targets. The purpose of this was that the skill aspect of PvE changed from ‘Piloting’ to ‘Gearing’. The highest level targets require you have the specific Gear that is correct for the situation, typically at the highest level. It is less important to have “necessary” Gear at lower-levels, creating a more balanced progression in the game. Doing this also allowed us to tune targets to create challenges, where player skill (aka piloting) is still a prominent aspect of the PvE experience. A good pilot can mitigate repair costs, especially in comparison to auto-ing those same targets.\n\n\nThe second step in refocusing the game direction to support end-game content was to increase and normalize the pace of new content. VEGA Conflict is a free-to-play game where it is absolutely possible for a player to play VEGA Conflict for free. A large portion of our vibrant community are players who don’t spend a dime on the game and we 100% value their presence. It is possible to get all the tech, hit all the targets, and do everything that a player who “coins” can...they just can’t do it right away. Time is the only gate in VEGA Conflict and that’s the limitation we’ve put in place by increasing the pace of content: if you want to compete at the very highest level of PvE from the very first moment new content is released, you are going to need the right Gear equipped. At the cadence at which we are releasing content, this means you may have to coin a little if you want to use this tech right away. You can still earn the necessary tech over time. You’ll get there eventually!\n\n\n\nWe’re already seeing a healthy response to these steps in the level of engagement in the game. We hope to continue to iterate on how content is released and stabilizing the game to increase the longevity of VEGA Conflict for years to come. &nbsp;\n\n\nWe understand that this topic is the primary point of frustration among you. It’s certainly a departure from how VEGA Conflict has historically released content.
