This supports the President’s vision to remove the guesswork in Philippine farming, first by providing color-coded guide maps that identify the areas where crops could be ideally grown based on soil types, climatic conditions and bio-physical requirements. This initiative is also intended to ensure food security and reduce poverty incidence in the agriculture and fisheries sector and the whole country.</p>\n<p>The first of the planning tools developed under the program is the <strong>AMIA National Color-Coded Agricultural Guide (NCCAG) Map</strong>. The color-coded map identifies the crops that are most suitable in agricultural parcels, and overlays soil properties, elevation, rainfall pattern, temperature and more importantly, the projected climate-induced multi-hazards. The maps will guide the government in determining forward-looking policies and site-specific food production projects and infrastructure investments. As a decision support tool, the color-coded map can be used for investment planning, climate-resilient research and development, innovative credit and insurance packages, climate-resilient agriculture and fisheries extension, infrastructure and disaster management. It will be useful for LGUs in preparing their CLUPs, planning disaster risk reduction measures and climate change adaptation technologies and practices.</p>\n<p>The AMIA-NCCAG map is scheduled to be presented by DA Secretary Emmanuel F. Piñol to President Rodrigo R. Duterte in a simple launching ceremony in Malacañang on March 7, 2017. Afterwhich, the color-coded maps will be made accessible to everyone in an interactive format uploaded in the internet. As the color-coded map is in its beta version, incorporation of other relevant data will further enrich its utility as a decision support tool.&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong><a
