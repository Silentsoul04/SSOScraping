this year, email marketing could become the \n      \"silver lining \" for many marketers.&nbsp;&nbsp; But how do you create a successful \n      email marketing campaign if you do not have a substantial email database?&nbsp; \n      That question may be one of the largest Internet related challenges facing \n      companies this year.</p>\n      <p>Research shows that the majority of organizations have email addresses \n      for less than 10% of their postal files.&nbsp; So how do you level the playing \n      field and start to add quality permission - based email addresses to your \n      database?</p>\n      <p>Here are some tips that you can use to cost effectively build your \n      email address database and increase your customer participation and \n      retention rates:&nbsp; </p>\n      <p><br>\n      START TO ASK FOR IT!&nbsp;&nbsp; </p>\n      <p>Every communication or touch point with a customer should start or end \n      with a request for an email address.&nbsp;&nbsp; By utilizing the four points below, \n      you should be able to add email addresses for 5% to 10% of your postal \n      file over the course of one year.</p>\n      <p>1.&nbsp; Direct Mail Collection</p>\n      <p>Think about how much time and money you spent for copy and design on \n      your last new direct mail piece.&nbsp; Most companies have started to ask their \n      customers for their email address information within these mailings.&nbsp; This \n      is a great step forward. However, companies need to look at one major \n      improvement if they want to increase their email address collection \n      rates.&nbsp; </p>\n      <p>To date, most requests for email address information have been pushed, \n      shoved or jammed into whatever white space remains.&nbsp; It should be no \n      surprise that the success rate has been less than stellar.</p>\n      <p>To improve on these efforts, you need to provide your members with a \n      reason to release their email addresses to you.&nbsp; E-newsletters, purchase \n      confirmations, petitions, and special discounts and offers are but a few \n      of the benefits that will encourage your members to come on board. </p>\n      <p>2.&nbsp; Web Page Collection</p>\n      <p>Many companies have an email address collection function in place via \n      the web.&nbsp; To improve your sign-up rates, add text below the email request \n      box that informs your visitors of the special email benefits that they \n      will receive (i.e. e-newsletters, purchase confirmations, delivery \n      updates, etc.) upon registering.&nbsp; You can also utilize a pop-up link to \n      inform users of these special benefits.</p>\n      <p>Finally, your email address request function should be available on \n      your home page. Don't make your users go and look for it.&nbsp; Every click \n      away from your home page reduces the chances of your users taking an \n      action and providing you with their email address information.</p>\n      <p>3.&nbsp; Existing Email Database Collection</p>\n      <p>Don't forget to ask the members of your existing customer base for \n      email addresses of their friends, family and associates.&nbsp; Viral marketing \n      is a powerful tool to use and is extremely cost effective!&nbsp; You could ask \n      them either to provide you with additional addresses or simply to pass on \n      your newsletter, email specific offers, or other information to others \n      they feel have similar interests. </p>\n      <p>4.&nbsp; Telemarketing Collection </p>\n      <p>Don't assume that your telemarketing agents are asking for email \n      addresses from potential customers.&nbsp; Ensure that your agents have an \n      updated script, which outlines the previously described benefits to \n      potential customers of providing their email addresses.</p>\n      <p>The suggestions above are a great start!&nbsp; Yet they really should be \n      viewed as a secondary plan for building your email database.&nbsp; To \n      exponentially and expeditiously grow your email database, please read on!</p>\n      <p><br>\n      EMAIL APPENDING&nbsp;&nbsp; </p>\n      <p>Utilizing an email appending service enables you to add email addresses \n      for up to 25% of your postal file, all within 3-4 weeks.</p>\n      <p>Email Appending - is the process of adding an individual's email \n      address to that individual's postal record in side your existing \n      database.&nbsp; This is accomplished by matching the postal database against a \n      third party, permission based database of postal and email address \n      information. </p>\n      <p>Best Practices - Email Appending is not a prospecting tool. The DMA \n      (Direct Marketing Association) and its interactive arm AIM (Association of \n      Interactive Marketing) have guidelines in place that dictate that email \n      appending only be used to append email addresses to your existing opt-in \n      postal record house file. </p>\n      <p>The Process - Your opt-in postal file is securely transferred to an \n      email appending provider, who will do an initial gross email address match \n      of your file against its opt-in database of postal and email address \n      records.&nbsp; Your appending provider will then send these matches a \n      permission-based message prepared by you.&nbsp; All bounces and opt-out \n      requests will be removed from the list.&nbsp; At that point a valid \n      permission-based email address file of your customers will be delivered \n      back to you.&nbsp;
