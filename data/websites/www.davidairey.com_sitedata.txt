.entry-header -->\n\n\t<div class=\"entry-content\">\n\t\t<p>For the third year running, <a href=\"http://www.twitter.com/johnmaeda\">John Maeda</a> released his <a href=\"https://designintechreport.wordpress.com\">Design in Tech Report</a>.</p>\n<p>The full PDF’s on the report site, with audio from the SXSW presentation on March 12th on <a href=\"https://soundcloud.com/johnmaeda/design-in-tech-audio\">SoundCloud</a>. The audio’s on <a href=\"https://vimeo.com/208035080\">Vimeo</a> with slides, too (below).</p>\n<p><iframe src=\"https://player.vimeo.com/video/208035080?color=ffffff&amp;title=0&amp;byline=0&amp;portrait=0\" width=\"640\" height=\"360\" frameborder=\"0\" webkitallowfullscreen=\"\" mozallowfullscreen=\"\" allowfullscreen=\"\"></iframe></p>\n<p>Of note…</p>\n<p>Top business schools are including a lot more design (thinking) classes, driven by market demand. That’s good for designers.</p>\n<p>Computational designers, in particular, are in demand (I’d not heard of the job title, and this is a good read that’s unrelated to the report: <a href=\"https://m.signalvnoise.com/the-unnecessary-fragmentation-of-design-jobs-d5b9c8621082\">The Unnecessary Fragmentation of Design Jobs</a>). These are the designers that big business (Facebook, Google, Amazon) are looking for. Computational design was pioneered by four women, Gillian Crampton-Smith, <a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joy_Mountford\">Joy Mountford</a>, and the late <a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muriel_Cooper\">Murial Cooper</a> and <a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_Burns\">Red Burns</a>.</p>\n<p>Designer communities (not that there are too many) are in demand. This year <a href=\"http://www.deviantart.com\">DeviantArt</a> was acquired by <a href=\"http://www.wix.com\">Wix</a>, and <a href=\"https://dribbble.com\">Dribbble</a> was acquired by <a href=\"http://tiny.website\">Tiny</a>. It was back in 2012 when <a href=\"https://www.behance.net\">Behance</a> was acquired by <a href=\"http://www.adobe.com\">Adobe</a>.</p>\n<p>27% of the internet is powered by WordPress.</p>\n<p>Creativity comes from difference.</p>\n<p>China has 500,000 design graduates every year. What’s happening there is very advanced.</p>\n<p>8 of the top 10 smartphone apps are owned by Facebook or Google. You think you have choice, but you don’t.</p>\n<p>There’s no mention of VR, but it’ll be included next year. A big addition, I expect.</p>\n<p>I must read the 2016 report, <a href=\"https://blog.marvelapp.com/state-of-the-digital-nation-2016/\">State of the Digital Nation</a>, by Jules Ehrhardt.</p>\n\t\t\t</div><!-- .entry-content -->\n\n</article><!-- #post-## -->\n\t\t\t\t<nav class=\"navigation post-navigation\">\n\t\t<h2 class=\"screen-reader-text\">Post
