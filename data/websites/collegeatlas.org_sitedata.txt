higher degree levels consistently have higher salaries and lower unemployment rates? The Bureau of Labor Statistics shows that graduates earning an associates degree can earn almost 20% more a year than those with only a high school diploma. Additionally, those with a bachelor's degree earn almost 43% more than those with an associates, and those with a master's degree earn almost 20% more than those with a bachelor's. The truth is, the more you experience and learn, the more opportunities you have to earn.</p><h4>The Rise of Online Education</h4><p>With the advancement of technology and online learning platforms by online colleges and even traditional schools, people everywhere are increasingly embracing the flexibility and positives that come with online classes and degree programs. <a href=\"https://www.collegeatlas.org/online-degrees.html\">Online degrees</a> are becomming more widely accepted. As the national standard for quality and accreditation are met and exceeded by online colleges and traditional colleges offering online classes and hybrid programs, that acceptance will continue to grow. In fact, in a survey done by the online institution Excelsior College and <em>Zobgy International</em>, 61% of CEOs and small business owners nationwide said they are familiar with online and distance learning programs. Of those executives, 83% said that an online degree is actually as credible as one earned through an on-campus, traditional college. A study done by the <em>Online Learning Consortium</em>, confirmed the same acceptance trend, as their study showed that over 69% of chief academic leaders said online learning is critical to their long-term strategy. Seventy-seven percent of those leaders also rated learning outcomes from online education to be the same, if not superior, to those of traditional campus classrooms.</p><h4>Start Your Education Path</h4><p>All types of degrees equal, there seems to be a \"best way to learn\" for every individual student. Online colleges, distance learning programs, and hybrid programs give every student the flexibility to learn and earn their degree. The trick is to find out what your \"best way to learn\" is and get started.
