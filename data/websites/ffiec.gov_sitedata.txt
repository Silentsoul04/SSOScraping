\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\t<!-- Text container -->\n\t\t\t\t<div class=\"contentbox-container\">\n\t\t\t\t\t<div class=\"contentbox-full\">\n\t\t\t \t\t\t<div class=\"contentbox-title-shading-small bg-blue12 box-on\">&nbsp;</div>\n\t\t\t\t\t\t<div class=\"contentbox-title-shading-small bg-blue12\">FFIEC Council </div>\n        \t\t        \n                <div class=\"contentbox-noshading\">\n                <p>The Council is a formal interagency body empowered to prescribe uniform principles, standards, and report forms for the federal examination of financial institutions by the Board of Governors of the Federal Reserve System  (<a href=\"http://www.federalreserve.gov\">FRB</a>), the Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation (<a href=\"http://www.fdic.gov\">FDIC</a>), the National Credit Union Administration (<a href=\"http://www.ncua.gov\">NCUA</a>), the Office of the Comptroller of the Currency (<a href=\"http://www.occ.treas.gov\">OCC</a>), and the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (<a href=\"http://www.consumerfinance.gov/\">CFPB</a>), and to make recommendations to promote uniformity in the supervision of financial institutions. In 2006, the State Liaison Committee (<a href=\"slc.htm\">SLC</a>) was added to the Council as a voting member. The SLC includes representatives from the Conference of State Bank Supervisors (<a href=\"http://www.csbs.org\">CSBS</a>), the American Council of State Savings Supervisors (<a href=\"http://www.ACSSS.org\">ACSSS</a>), and the National Association of State Credit Union Supervisors (<a href=\"http://www.nascus.org/\">NASCUS</a>).  </p>\n               \n\t\t\t\t  </div>\n
