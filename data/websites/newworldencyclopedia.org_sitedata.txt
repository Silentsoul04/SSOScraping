They traditionally speak the Potawatomi language, a member of the Algonquian family.</span>\n<p><span style=\"font-size:92%;\">The Potawatomi controlled a vast amount of territory in the 1700s and served as middlemen for the fur trade between the French and various Great Lakes Tribes. Among the first Native Americans to intermarry with the Europeans, they fought alongside the <a href=\"/entry/France\" title=\"France\">French</a> in the <a href=\"/entry/French_and_Indian_Wars\" title=\"French and Indian Wars\" class=\"mw-redirect\">French and Indian Wars</a> and later as allies of the <a href=\"/entry/England\" title=\"England\">British</a> in the <a href=\"/entry/War_of_1812\" title=\"War of 1812\">War of 1812</a>.</span></p>\n<p><span style=\"font-size:92%;\">Descendants numbers between 30,000 to 40,000 in the early twenty-first century, scattered throughout <a href=\"/entry/Canada\" title=\"Canada\">Canada</a> and the <a href=\"/entry/United_States\" title=\"United States\">United States</a>, with many settled on or near the ten (official and unofficial) reservations. Most of today's Potawatomi also claim European descendancy.</span></p>\n</div>\n<div class=\"infobox\" style=\"background-color:white; width:95%; padding:0.5em; margin-left:0; margin-right:1em;\">\n<h2><span class=\"mw-headline\" id=\"Popular_Article:_Matchmaking\">Popular Article: <a href=\"/entry/Matchmaking\" title=\"Matchmaking\" class=\"mw-redirect\">Matchmaking</a></span></h2>\n<p><span style=\"font-size:92%;\"><b><a href=\"/entry/Matchmaking\" title=\"Matchmaking\" class=\"mw-redirect\">Matchmaking</a></b> is the process of introducing a couple as potential partners in <a href=\"/entry/Marriage\" title=\"Marriage\">marriage</a>. People in diverse <a href=\"/entry/Culture\" title=\"Culture\">cultures</a>, past and present, have sought assistance from matchmakers because they may have a deeper understanding of human character, a wider connection to acquaintances, and greater knowledge and experience to help someone choose a marriage partner. The increase in popularity of \"love matches\" based on romantic and physical attraction, together with a loosening of the restrictions on behavior and decline in <a href=\"/entry/Arranged_marriage\" title=\"Arranged marriage\">arranged marriages</a>, led to a decline in the use of matchmakers with young people turning to various social situations to find prospective partners. <a href=\"/entry/Technology\" title=\"Technology\">Technological</a> advances, however, have seen the re-emergence of the matchmaking process, as <a href=\"/entry/Computer\" title=\"Computer\">computers</a> and the <a href=\"/entry/Internet\" title=\"Internet\">internet</a> became popular tools in the search for an ideal mate. Ultimately, though, the involvement of more than technology is necessary to guide people to find a partner with whom they can build a harmonious relationship leading to a loving <a href=\"/entry/Family\" title=\"Family\">family</a>, based on not only the physical but also the spiritual aspects of their lives.</span></p>\n<p><span style=\"font-size:92%;\">Historically, in many <a href=\"/entry/Culture\" title=\"Culture\">cultures</a> parents would request the assistance of a matchmaker in finding a suitable spouse for their child. The job of the matchmaker was extremely important because dating and free choice of a marriage partner was not allowed, and the only way for young people marry was by <a href=\"/entry/Arranged_marriage\" title=\"Arranged marriage\">arranged marriage</a>. For many centuries, the matchmaker's job was to check the ethnic identity and compatibility of the proposed couple. They could also act as \"middlemen\" by introducing potential candidates, especially if the acquaintances of the parents and family were limited.</span></p>\n</div>\n</div>\n<div class=\"large-3
