Purpose</h2>\n\n<p>We think of this wiki as our <strong>lab book</strong> that we happen to keep open for all to see.</p>\n\n<div class=\"standout\">\n<p>The <strong>purpose of the <math xmlns=\"http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\" class=\"maruku-mathml\" display=\"inline\"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding=\"application/x-tex\">n</annotation></semantics></math>Lab</strong> is to provide a public place where people can make notes about stuff. The purpose is not to make polished expositions of material; that is a happy by-product.</p>\n\n<p>We all make notes as we read papers, read books and doodle on pads of paper. The <math xmlns=\"http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\" class=\"maruku-mathml\" display=\"inline\"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding=\"application/x-tex\">n</annotation></semantics></math>Lab is somewhere to put all those notes, and, incidentally, to make them available to others. Others might read them and add or polish them. But even if they don’t, it is still easier to link from them to other notes that you’ve made.</p>\n</div>\n\n<p>For more see</p>\n\n<ul>\n<li>\n<p><a class=\"existingWikiWord\" href=\"/schreiber/show/What+is...+the+nLab\" title=\"schreiber\">What is... the nLab</a>?</p>\n</li>\n\n<li>\n<p><a class=\"existingWikiWord\" href=\"/nlab/show/About\">About</a>.</p>\n</li>\n\n<li>\n<p>Wikipedia entry: <em><a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NLab\">nLab</a></em></p>\n</li>\n</ul>\n\n<h2 id=\"Discussion\">2. Discussion, Comments, Questions</h2>\n\n<p>While we work on the <math xmlns=\"http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\" class=\"maruku-mathml\" display=\"inline\"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding=\"application/x-tex\">n</annotation></semantics></math>Lab, we talk to each other on the <a href=\"http://nforum.ncatlab.org\">nForum</a>. In particular, for all but the most trivial edits (correcting spelling or punctuation, etc.), we make a note of our latest edits to the <math xmlns=\"http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\" class=\"maruku-mathml\" display=\"inline\"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding=\"application/x-tex\">n</annotation></semantics></math>Lab in the part <a href=\"http://nforum.ncatlab.org/5/\">nForum – latest changes</a>, where they may be discussed further.</p>\n\n<p>If you do make <strong>contributions</strong> to the <math xmlns=\"http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\" class=\"maruku-mathml\" display=\"inline\"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding=\"application/x-tex\">n</annotation></semantics></math>Lab, you are strongly encouraged to similarly drop a short note there about what you have done – or maybe just about what you plan to do or even what you would like others to do. See <em><a class=\"existingWikiWord\" href=\"/nlabmeta/show/Welcome+to+the+nForum\" title=\"nlabmeta\">Welcome to the nForum</a></em> for more information.</p>\n\n<p>If you do not want to contribute to the <math xmlns=\"http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\" class=\"maruku-mathml\" display=\"inline\"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding=\"application/x-tex\">n</annotation></semantics></math>Lab, but if you have <strong>comments</strong> on an entry – say because you are an expert and feel that information is wrong or missing – or <strong>questions</strong> – say because you are a layperson and feel that things could be explained better – then we generally prefer that you post that comment or question to the <a href=\"http://nforum.ncatlab.org\">nForum</a>, where it is visible to everybody who might be concerned.</p>\n\n<p>In case that you do feel that this is not an option and that you do need to contact privately (say by email) a single author of an <math xmlns=\"http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\" class=\"maruku-mathml\" display=\"inline\"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding=\"application/x-tex\">n</annotation></semantics></math>Lab page, please make sure that you know who the right author is. Beware that the <math xmlns=\"http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\" class=\"maruku-mathml\" display=\"inline\"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding=\"application/x-tex\">n</annotation></semantics></math>Lab pages are visibly “signed” only by the name of the last person who made any edit on the page, no matter how minor. To find the author who made the edit that you want to comment or ask about privately, you should click on the link “History” at the bottom of any page to see which version was authored by whom.</p>\n\n<h2 id=\"Contributing\">3. Contributing to the <math xmlns=\"http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\" class=\"maruku-mathml\" display=\"inline\"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding=\"application/x-tex\">n</annotation></semantics></math>Lab</h2>\n\n<p>If after looking around for a while you feel like contributing yourself, you are welcome to do so. But read <a class=\"existingWikiWord\" href=\"/nlab/show/About\">About</a> to be sure you understand what we are doing here (to the extent that we understand this ourselves, at least). If you feel unsure about appropriate content, see <a class=\"existingWikiWord\" href=\"/nlab/show/what+to+contribute\">What to Contribute</a>. For technical hints see <a class=\"existingWikiWord\" href=\"/nlab/show/HowTo\">HowTo</a>.</p>\n\n<p>If you make any edits to the <math xmlns=\"http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\" class=\"maruku-mathml\" display=\"inline\"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding=\"application/x-tex\">n</annotation></semantics></math>Lab, please inform the rest of the <math xmlns=\"http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\" class=\"maruku-mathml\" display=\"inline\"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding=\"application/x-tex\">n</annotation></semantics></math>Lab community by dropping a brief message in the <em>latest changes</em> section of the <a href=\"http://nforum.ncatlab.org\">nForum</a>!</p>\n\n<h2 id=\"TermsOfUse\">4. Using the <math xmlns=\"http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\" class=\"maruku-mathml\" display=\"inline\"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding=\"application/x-tex\">n</annotation></semantics></math>Lab</h2>\n\n<p>One goal of the <math xmlns=\"http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\" class=\"maruku-mathml\" display=\"inline\"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding=\"application/x-tex\">n</annotation></semantics></math>Lab is to help make information widely available and usefully related to other information. In this users and contributors are expected to follow traditional academic practice:</p>\n\n<ul>\n<li>\n<p>Using and distributing content obtained from the <math xmlns=\"http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\" class=\"maruku-mathml\" display=\"inline\"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding=\"application/x-tex\">n</annotation></semantics></math>Lab is free and encouraged if you acknowledge the source, as usual in academia.</p>\n\n<p>(There is currently no consensus on a more formal license statement, but if it matters check if relevant individual contributors state such on their nLab homepages.)</p>\n\n<p>If you cite a page you may want to point to a specific version of it, because <math xmlns=\"http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\" class=\"maruku-mathml\" display=\"inline\"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding=\"application/x-tex\">n</annotation></semantics></math>Lab pages can change. You can find a list of all the versions of a page by clicking on the <strong>History</strong> link at the bottom of the page itself.</p>\n</li>\n\n<li>\n<p>Conversely, any content contributed to the <math xmlns=\"http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\" class=\"maruku-mathml\" display=\"inline\"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding=\"application/x-tex\">n</annotation></semantics></math>Lab is publicly available and you should be aware that others may use your contributions (whatever you decide to do with their content elsewhere) and indeed may edit them. In the first case you trust that users will cite your contributions properly, in the second that they will respect and only improve on them. At the same time, you are expected to properly acknowledge sources of information for material entered into the <math xmlns=\"http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\" class=\"maruku-mathml\" display=\"inline\"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding=\"application/x-tex\">n</annotation></semantics></math>Lab.</p>\n</li>\n</ul>\n\n<p>Usually this works well. If there is need for discussion, the <a href=\"http://nforum.ncatlab.org\">nForum</a> is the forum to turn to. If serious problems arise, the <a href=\"#SteeringCommitte\">steering committee</a> might intervene.</p>\n\n<h2 id=\"SoftwareRequirements\">5. Software requirements</h2>\n\n<p>The <math xmlns=\"http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\" class=\"maruku-mathml\" display=\"inline\"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding=\"application/x-tex\">n</annotation></semantics></math>Lab sends mathematical formulas to the browser using <a href=\"http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MathML\">MathML</a>.</p>\n\n<p>Notice that <strong>you don’t need to know any MathML</strong> for editing the <math xmlns=\"http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\" class=\"maruku-mathml\" display=\"inline\"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding=\"application/x-tex\">n</annotation></semantics></math>Lab, only your browser does. You write formulas into the <math xmlns=\"http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\" class=\"maruku-mathml\" display=\"inline\"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding=\"application/x-tex\">n</annotation></semantics></math>Lab between dollar signs in <a href=\"http://golem.ph.utexas.edu/~distler/blog/itex2MMLcommands.html\">iTeX</a>, which is very similar to ordinary <a href=\"https://www.latex-project.org/\">LaTeX</a>.</p>\n\n<p>Presently only <a href=\"http://www.mozilla.com/firefox/\">Firefox</a> and its derivatives have implemented <em>native</em> rendering of MathML. Presently all other browsers fall back to invoking <a href=\"https://www.mathjax.org/\">MathJax</a>. This works fine on small pages, but on pages with substantial content the MathJax rendering takes up to several minutes.</p>\n\n<p>This means that presently <strong>you should use Firefox</strong> or its derivatives to view the <math xmlns=\"http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\" class=\"maruku-mathml\" display=\"inline\"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding=\"application/x-tex\">n</annotation></semantics></math>Lab (<a href=\"https://www.mozilla.org/en-GB/firefox/new/?gclid=CJnjs-3R3M0CFRHhGwodA10Euw\">free download of Firefox</a>).</p>\n\n<h2 id=\"Server\">6.
