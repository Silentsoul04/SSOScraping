block-region-content block-1\">  \n      <h2 class=\"block-title\">The Next Phase for the Digital Media Law Project</h2>\n  \n  <p>After seven years of providing legal assistance to independent journalism through various methods, the Digital Media Law Project will soon spin off its most effective initiatives and cease operation as a stand-alone project within the Berkman Center. Having successfully developed a range of tools to meet the legal needs of online media, we are taking this step to ensure that our work continues in a robust and sustainable fashion.</p>\n<p><a href=\"http://www.dmlp.org/blog/2014/seven-years-serving-and-studying-legal-needs-digital-journalism\">Read more here</a> about the upcoming changes and the future of the DMLP's work.</p>\n<!-- OLD ITEMS AND PENDING ITEMS\n\n<b>A New Report on Media Credentialing Practices in the United States</b>\n<p>\nThe Digital Media Law Project is pleased to present a new report in conjunction with Journalist's Resource, <a href=\"http://www.dmlp.org/credentials\"><i>Who Gets a Press Pass? Media Credentialing Practices in the United States</i></a>. \n</p>\n<p>\nMedia credentials are an essential tool for covering restricted events and places in the United States, but inconsistencies and gaps in existing standards for the issuance of credentials have created substantial confusion as to which journalists are entitled to special access. <i>Who Gets a Press Pass?</i> presents a first-of-its-kind analysis of this complex environment, surveying different legal regimes for media credentials throughout the country as well as the actual experiences of more than 1,300 journalists who have sought credentials from federal, state, local, and private organizations. The survey reveals a diverse environment, but one where certain categories of journalists have a significant advantage over others in obtaining credentials. \n</p>\n<p>\n<i>Who Gets a Press Pass? </i>is a report of the Media Credentialing Working Group, including the <a href=\"http://www.dmlp.org\" target=\"_blank\">Digital Media Law Project</a>, <a href=\"http://journalistsresource.org/\" target=\"_blank\">Journalist's Resource</a>, <a href=\"http://www.freepress.net/\" target=\"_blank\">Free Press</a>, the <a href=\"https://nppa.org/\" target=\"_blank\">National Press Photographers Association</a>, the <a href=\"http://investigativenewsnetwork.org/\" target=\"_blank\">Investigative News Network</a>, and the <a href=\"http://www.niemanlab.org/\">Nieman Journalism Lab</a>. These organizations are deeply concerned about the effect of credentialing systems on all members of the journalism world, including both employees of journalism organizations and those who work independently. \n</p>\n<p>\nIt is our hope that this report will help those studying and practicing in the new online environment for journalism to better understand this new world. You can read the executive summary and access the full text of the report <a target=\"_blank\" href=\"/credentials\">here</a>, and read our press release about this report <a target=\"_blank\" href=\"/blog/2014/dmlp-announcement-new-report-media-credentialing-united-states\">here</a>. \n</p>\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n<b>A New Report on the Clients of the Online Media Legal Network</b>\n<p>\nThe Digital Media Law Project is pleased to present a new report, <a href=\"http://www.dmlp.org/omln500\"><i>The Legal Needs of Emerging Online Media: The Online Media Legal Network after 500 Referrals</i></a>. \n</p>\n<p>\nOver the last four years, the Digital Media Law Project has had the privilege to work with a dedicated group of lawyers throughout the United States to provide pro bono legal services to a growing community of independent journalists and other media ventures online. Through our attorney referral service, the <a target=\"_blank\" href=\"http://www.omln.org\">Online Media Legal Network</a>, we have helped more than 260 clients with over 500 separate legal matters. This experience has provided the staff of the DMLP with a unique perspective on a substantial cross-section of the digital journalism environment. \n</p>\n<p>\nNow, to mark the milestone of our 500th referral, we have prepared a report on our clients and their legal needs. This report describes the operation of the Online Media Legal Network, the nature of its client base, and the legal questions that they have faced. While this survey of our clients and their matters raises some cause for concern, it also provides substantial cause for hope that digital journalism is evolving into a diverse and vibrant ecosystem of participants that are proactive about their legal needs. \n</p>\n<p>\nIt is our hope that this report will help those studying and practicing in the new online environment for journalism to better understand this new world. You can read the executive summary and access the full text of the report <a target=\"_blank\" href=\"/omln500\">here</a>, and read our blog post about the preparation of this report <a target=\"_blank\" href=\"/blog/2014/service-and-research-frontier-media-law\">here</a>. \n</p>\n\n<b>New Resources for Non-Profit Journalism</b>\n<p>\nThe Digital Media Law Project is pleased to announce the launch of <a href=\"/irs\" target=\"_blank\">new and updated resources</a> for journalism non-profit organizations seeking tax-exempt status from the Internal Revenue Service.\n</p>\n<p>\nFollowing the disruption of commercial revenue models for journalism, gaps have opened in local and topical coverage by for-profit news organizations. New non-profit ventures are seeking to respond to the needs of their communities; however, in order to avoid payment of corporate tax, and to receive foundation funding and tax-deductible public donations, these ventures need to be recognized by the IRS as tax-exempt under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code. \n</p>\n<p>\nApplying for a Section 501(c)(3) tax exemption can be an extended and complex process. To help non-profit journalism organizations prepare for IRS scrutiny, we have provided a collection of resources including: a video debunking myths and misconceptions about tax-exempt journalism; a checklist for whether tax-exempt status may be right for your venture; a breakdown of the IRS application process; a detailed guide to the standards used by the IRS to evaluate news organizations; and an archive of application materials filed by journalism non-profits that successfully obtained Section 501(c)(3) status. It is our hope that these resources will assist news organizations to understand the IRS process and avoid common pitfalls that can delay an application for months. You can begin using these resources <a href=\"/irs\" target=\"_blank\">here</a>, and read more about our interest in non-profit journalism <a href=\"/blog/2014/new-approach-helping-journalism-non-profits-irs\" target=\"_blank\">here</a>.\n</p>\n<p>\n<b> UPDATE: The online chat session scheduled for April 10, 2014, has been postponed; please stay tuned here for a new date. In the meantime, check out our <a href=\"/irs\" target=\"_blank\">new resources</a>, and feel free to <a href=\"/contact\" target=\"_blank\">contact</a> us with any questions.</b>\n</p>\n\n\n<b>We Have a New Name!</b>\n<p>\nWe are pleased to announce that the Citizen Media Law Project has now transitioned to its new name, the Digital Media Law Project!\n</p>\n<p>\nThe Citizen Media Law Project was created in 2007 to support the vibrant online culture of citizen media and independent journalism by providing free legal advice and information on a wide range of media law, intellectual property and business law issues. Since then, there has been an explosion of novel online journalism and publishing ventures, with the development of new ways of bringing information of critical public importance to light. Experiments abound as innovators in the journalism space attempt to fill the information gap left by the contraction of traditional news organizations. These experiments often originate from citizen journalists attempting to improve their own communities, but they are as likely to come from professional journalists seeking a new voice through independent online ventures.\n</p>\n<p>\nThe expansion and complexity of the online publishing environment makes the change from “Citizen” to “Digital” a natural progression, reflecting both our recognition of the role of both citizens and professionals in online journalism and the expansion of our services.  Since our launch, we have developed a coordinated suite of legal resources to address a journalism venture’s specific needs. Our networks of attorneys, clients and scholars serve as an early warning system that allows the project to respond to breaking legal issues affecting journalism. Our online legal guide provides detailed information on a wide range of media and business law topics, and our threats database tracks legal actions against online speech. Our 50-state pro bono lawyer referral network, the Online Media Legal Network, provides experienced counsel when direct representation is needed. \n</p>\n<p>\nRead more about our change of name <a target=\"_blank\" href=\"/blog/2013/we-are-digital-media-law-project\">here</a>, and be sure to follow us on Twitter with our new handle, <a href=\"http://www.twitter.com/dmlpberkman\">@DMLPBerkman</a>!\n</p><p><b>Are you a journalist or online publisher in need of legal assistance?</b> Please visit our sister site, the <a href=\"http://www.omln.org/\">Online Media Legal Network</a> (OMLN), a free initiative that connects lawyers from across the country with online journalists and digital media creators who need legal help. Lawyers participating in OMLN provide qualifying online publishers with <i>pro bono</i> and reduced fee legal assistance on a broad range of legal issues. More information is available <a href=\"http://www.omln.org/\">here</a>.\n</p><div style=\"text-align: center\"><a href=\"http://www.omln.org/\"><img src=\"/sites/citmedialaw.org/files/OMLN boxv4.jpg\" align=\"center\" width=\"264\" height=\"81\" /></a></div><p><strong>New Resource Devoted to Section 230 of the CDA</strong>: CMLP launched a <a href=\"/section-230\" target=\"_blank\">page</a> that aggregates everything on our site relating to  <a href=\"/legal-guide/immunity-online-publishers-under-communications-decency-act\">section 230 of the Communications Decency Act</a> (&quot;Section 230&quot;), the important federal statute that protects operators of websites and other interactive computer services from liability for publishing the statements of third-parties. The page includes detailed background on Section 230, links to our <a href=\"/legal-guide\">legal guide</a> and feeds showing recent legal threats from our <a href=\"/database\">database</a>, <a href=\"/blog\">blog posts</a>, and news from across the Web. You can check it out <a href=\"/section-230\">here</a>.</p>\n<p><strong>Citizen Media Law Project Leads <i>Amicus</i> Effort Promoting Rights of Anonymous Speakers</strong>: In a case involving important First Amendment rights, the CMLP joined a number of media and advocacy organizations, including Gannett Co., Hearst Corporation, Illinois Press Association, Online News Association, Public Citizen, Reporters Committee for Freedom of the Press, and Tribune Company, in asking an Illinois appellate court to protect the rights of anonymous speakers online by imposing procedural safeguards before requiring that their identities be disclosed. More information is available <a href=\"http://www.citmedialaw.org/blog/2009/citizen-media-law-project-and-berkman-cyberlaw-clinic-lead-amicus-effort-protect-anonymous\">here</a>.</p>\n<p><strong>Citizen Media Law Project Launches Legal Guide</strong>: CMLP's <a href=\"http://www.citmedialaw.org/legal-guide\">legal guide</a> is intended for use by citizen media creators with or without formal legal training and focuses on the wide range of legal issues citizen and online media are likely to face, including risks associated with publication, such as defamation and privacy torts; copyright; trademark; access to government information; newsgathering; and general legal issues involved in setting up a business and finding a web host. You can access the guide <a href=\"http://www.citmedialaw.org/legal-guide\">here</a>.</p>\n<p><strong>The Citizen Media Law Project is Hiring</strong>: The CMLP is looking to hire a <a href=\"http://www.citmedialaw.org/blog/2009/citizen-media-law-project-hiring-staff-attorney\">Staff Attorney/Resident Fellow</a> to assist with the development of a <i>pro bono</i> network of media lawyers and law school clinics to support online journalism and new media startups.  We're also seeking paid interns for <a href=\"/about/academic-year-internships\">part-time work during the spring semester</a> and for <a href=\"/about/summer-internships\">full-time work over the summer</a>.  Law students with media and/or cyberlaw experience, or a background in journalism, are especially encouraged to apply.</p>\n\n<p><strong>Documenting the 2009 Presidential Inauguration</strong>: If you are heading to Washington for the Inauguration, you should know that strict security measures will be in place across the Washington area, particularly where official events are taking place.
