These measures are based on the Décret Crit’Air of June 29, 2016, and on the traffic law. <br><br>The environmental zones introduced in France can be divided in two types: those zones where traffic is restricted and those where the goal is air protection. The zones where traffic is restricted are called “traffic restriction zones” (<a href=\"https://www.crit-air.fr/en/information-about-the-critair-vignette/french-environmental-zones-zcr/french-environmental-zones.html#c29521\" title=\"Opens internal link in current window\" class=\"internal-link\"><b>ZCR</b></a>, French acronym). The zones where the priority is air protection are called “air protection zones” (<a href=\"https://www.crit-air.fr/en/information-about-the-critair-vignette/french-environmental-zones-zcr/french-environmental-zones.html#c29522\" title=\"Opens internal link in current window\" class=\"internal-link\"><b>ZPA</b></a>, French acronym). <br><b><br>The ZCR environmental zones</b><br>The introduction of a ZCR in France is the responsibility of every city or municipality and is regulated by the national Décret YCR 2016-847 of June 28, 2016. After a 6-month period of hearings, which allows affected citizens to voice their concerns and take part in the decision-making process, the zone becomes binding. In order to drive in the cities and municipalities of France that have introduced an environmental zone ZCR, it is necessary to have a Crit’Air vignette, which consists of 6 different categories. More information and details about traffic norms can be found <a href=\"https://www.crit-air.fr/en/information-about-the-critair-vignette/french-environmental-zones-zcr/french-environmental-zones.html#c29521\" title=\"Opens internal link in current window\" class=\"internal-link\"><b>here</b></a>. <br><br><b>ZPA air protection zones</b><br>In contrast to the ZCR zones, the “air protection zones”— “Zones de protection de l’air” (ZPA) in French— are no permanent and only affect municipalities and big cities (“metropoles”, in French). ZPA zones are established for large cities (e.g., Grenoble) or for whole geographical areas (e.g., the Arve Valley).<br>These zones are only valid in case of an air pollution peak. This means that traffic restrictions will only be valid when established limits for fine particulates and nitrogen oxides are exceeded during several days. Depending on the level of pollution, it will be determined what vignette color can drive on what days through the zone. Norms can vary from ZPA zone to ZPA zone and from city to city. As soon as the weather improves, restrictions in the air protection zone will not be valid anymore. More information and details about traffic norms can be found <a href=\"https://www.crit-air.fr/en/information-about-the-critair-vignette/french-environmental-zones-zcr/french-environmental-zones.html#c29522\" title=\"Opens internal link in current window\" class=\"internal-link\"><b>here</b></a>.</p></div></div></div> </div> </div> </div> </div> <div class=\"outer\"> <div class=\"inner\"> <div class=\"content\"> <div class=\"clearfix content_vierer\"> <div class=\"vierer_outer_gesamt\"><div class=\"vierer_outer\"><div class=\"vierer_inner\"><div class=\"bild\"><a href=\"https://www.crit-air.fr/en/information-about-the-critair-vignette/fine-dust-and-nox/fine-dust-and-nox.html#c16573\"><img src=\"https://www.crit-air.fr/fileadmin/_processed_/csm_Luftverschmutzung_da44e46f35.jpg\" alt=\"Air pollution\" title=\"Air pollution\" class=\"image-landscape\"></a></div><div class=\"ueberschrift\"><a href=\"https://www.crit-air.fr/en/information-about-the-critair-vignette/fine-dust-and-nox/fine-dust-and-nox.html#c16573\">Nitrogen Oxides and Fine Dust</a></div><div class=\"text\">Nitrogen Oxides and fine dust, particularly from diesel vehicles, are the reason why France introduced ZCR zones and the Crit'Air Vignette.
