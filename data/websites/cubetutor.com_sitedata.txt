This project has taken (and continues to take) a lot longer than I initially expected and I am very conscious that there has been little movement on the rest of the site in the meantime, despite the flurry of feature requests over on Champion Central. For example Mirror-Sigil Sergeant tokens.</p>\n</li>\n\t<li>\n<p align=\"LEFT\">Cards that ask you to <b>choose a creature type </b>and put a&nbsp;token of that type into play, for example <b>Riptide Replicator</b>.</p>\n</li>\n\t<li>\n<p align=\"LEFT\">Some older token types that never got a card will just show the card back image. If you have a cool custom image for a token that we are missing and image for, then please get in touch.</p>\n</li>\n</ul>\n<p align=\"LEFT\">The tokens that are detected will appear on your tokens page along with a list of cards that produce each token and the <b>number </b>of tokens produced by those cards. Some cards are straightforward, <b>Spectral Procession </b>produces three white spirits with flying and that's that. Other cards produce a variable number of tokens and we have two different ways of specifying that.</p>\n\n<ul>\n\t<li>\n<p align=\"LEFT\"><b>'N'</b> – These cards produce N tokens. For example Dragon Fodder is 2.</p>\n</li>\n\t<li>\n<p align=\"LEFT\"><b>'X'</b> – These cards have an effect that is likely to put multiple tokens into play either at once of over the course of several turns. Examples include Secure the Wastes, Bitterblossom and Ant Queen.</p>\n</li>\n\t<li>\n<p align=\"LEFT\">'<b>N+' ­</b>- These cards produce N tokens and <b>might</b> be able to put them into play more than once but it takes a bit more work than an <b>X</b> card. For example <b>Grave Titan</b> is a <b>2+</b> token producer as it may put 2 tokens into play more than once but it requires being able to attack. Planeswalkers that produce tokens are generally considered to be N+ cards as they have multiple modes and may not produce a token every turn.</p>\n</li>\n</ul>\n<p align=\"LEFT\">Whether a card is an N, X or N+ token producer is somewhat down to context and opinion, of course Dragon Fodder could produce 4 tokens with a Doubling Season in play, or a Snapcaster Mage but these sorts of cases are not considered for our purpose.</p>\nTwo players are assumed for all tokens that put a number of tokens into play based on the number of players.\n<p align=\"LEFT\">The final concept is the <b>Suggested Minimum</b> number of tokens that you should have available for gaming with your cube. For the purposes of calculating this number <b>X </b>token producers are assumed to produce 5 tokens and <b>N+</b> token producers are assumed to produce 2N tokens, e.g. the value for <b>Hero of Bladehold ­</b>is <b>4</b>.</p>\n<p align=\"LEFT\">Please bear in mind that this number is a suggested <b>minimum</b> not a suggested <b>optimal</b> number of tokens that you should have available!</p>\n<p align=\"LEFT\"><u><b>Thank You</b></u></p>\nFinally I would just like to reiterate my thanks to everyone who supports Cube Tutor.
