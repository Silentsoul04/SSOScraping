//-->\n\n<div class=\"postarea\">\n\n      <p>In the early days of the commercial Internet, domain names were free.</p>\n<p>One had to ask, in order to get, however, and the process required manual labor, along with the ability to circumvent the bureaucratic process imposed by the domain manager’s operators, including <a href=\"http://domaingang.com/domain-news/interview-from-1995-who-made-this-man-the-king/\" target=\"_blank\"><strong>Jon Postel.</strong></a></p>\n<p>Up until the summer of 1995 that domain names became intangible commodities with an $100 dollar price tag for the first two years of registration, the process of registering a single domain wasn’t easy.</p>\n<p>And yet, a 20 year old kid named <a href=\"http://links.net\" target=\"_blank\"><strong>Justin Hall</strong></a>, ventured off to apply for the registration of the domain <strong>Fuck.com.</strong></p>\n<p><strong>Why Fuck.com ?</strong></p>\n<p><img class=\"aligncenter size-full wp-image-49952\" src=\"http://domaingang.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/03/fuck-com.jpg\" alt=\"\" width=\"780\" height=\"255\" srcset=\"https://domaingang.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/03/fuck-com.jpg 780w, https://domaingang.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/03/fuck-com-300x98.jpg 300w, https://domaingang.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/03/fuck-com-768x251.jpg 768w, https://domaingang.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/03/fuck-com-150x49.jpg 150w\" sizes=\"(max-width: 780px) 100vw, 780px\"></p>\n<p>He wasn’t a pornographer, but seeing how someone had already registered BigTits.com, and Procter &amp; Gamble had grabbed Diarrhea.com, <strong>why not?</strong></p>\n<p>In all essence, “fuck” can have a myriad other uses, other than the obvious physical act; regardless, Justin Hall’s application for Fuck.com in late 1994 was scrutinized.</p>\n<p>A week after the September 1st, 1994 application for Fuck.com, Justin Hall received this response from InterNIC:</p>\n<blockquote><p><em>Justin, my friend – is this domain really necessary? or are you just pushing the envelope?? Perhaps the purpose of this domain could be realized as a third-level domain under either of your cyberogasmic domains???</em></p>\n<p><em>We’re not in the business of censoring names, but by the same token we are the stewards of the name space. There are undoubtedly a large number of people who would be offended by public display of this name. Seems to me there are already standards in place in other areas that could be applied here. For example, if you wanted to obtain a customized car license plate with various “4-letter” words, would your state issue one to you????</em></p>\n<p><em>For the time being, I am denying your application. By this message, I am asking for any comment that the project manager or IANA may have on the subject.</em></p>\n<p><em>I will update your email address and phone number in the InterNIC database.</em></p>\n<p><em>Regards, Duane</em><br>\n<em> InterNIC Domain Registration</em></p></blockquote>\n<p>Bureaucracy at work, one would say, and yet, Justin Hall wasn’t phased by it.</p>\n<p>He responded with gusto:</p>\n<blockquote><p><em>Thanks for taking the time to review my domain request individually. I understand your problem with the domain name, and so I wanted to write back to allay your fears. I have set up a small publishing company online and I would like to use this domain to point to a machine where I will be keeping the materials related to this company. This domain name fits in with my vision of the company perfectly.</em></p>\n<p><em>I don’t disgree with you that fuck is a powerful word – that’s why I would like to use it as a domain name for powerful press. The way the net/www works, people won’t see it unless someone makes a link to it, which makes that someone the first line of determining propriety. Plus, people can operate on the www with transparent URLs, never having to see the address behind the link. Finally, someone writing us e-mail at that address is doing so by their choice, and wouldn’t have the word foisted on them.</em></p>\n<p><em>With the ever expanding growth of the net, it was inevitable that someone would choose this name.
