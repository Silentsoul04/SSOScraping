href=\"http://www.skipprichard.com/miniposts/9-ways-to-make-your-customers-happy/\" target=\"_blank\">Consumer</a></strong></h2>\n<p>It’s time to rethink what you sell. And your customers. Don’t forget to rethink your marketing, your competition. And, don’t forget your teams.</p>\n<p>That’s the message from Tara-Nicholle Nelson, author of <a href=\"https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/1626568839/leaderinsigh-20\" class=\"\" target=\"_blank\"><em>The Transformational Consumer: Fuel a Lifelong Love Affair with Customers by Helping Them Get Healthier, Wealthier and Wiser</em></a>. She is the founder of Transformational Consumer Insights and the former VP of Marketing for MyFitnessPal, where she led the team that grew the platform to over 100 million customers.</p>\n<p>&nbsp;</p>\n\t\t\t<div class=\"tweetable tweetalign_center\" style=\"width:80%\"><b>“</b><blockquote>\n\t\t\t\t<div class=\"tweet\">\n\t\t\t\t\t<p><br>\n“The best..measure of innovation is change in human behavior.” -Stuart Butterfield<br>\n</p>\n\t\t\t\t</div>\t\t\t\t<ul class=\"actions\">\n\t\t\t\t\t<li><a href=\"https://twitter.com/intent/tweet?source=tweetbutton&amp;text=%E2%80%9CThe+best..measure+of+innovation+is+change+in+human+behavior.%22+-Stuart+Butterfield+https%3A%2F%2Fwww.skipprichard.com%2Ffuel-a-lifelong-love-affair-with-your-customers%2F&amp;via=skipprichard\"><i></i>Tweet This</a></li><li><a href=\"http://www.facebook.com/sharer.php?s=100&amp;p[url]=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.skipprichard.com%2Ffuel-a-lifelong-love-affair-with-your-customers%2F&amp;p[title]=Quote+from%3A+Fuel+a+Lifelong+Love+Affair+with+Your+Customers&amp;p[summary]=%E2%80%9CThe+best..measure+of+innovation+is+change+in+human+behavior.%22+-Stuart+Butterfield&amp;p[images][0]=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.skipprichard.com%2Fwp-content%2Fthemes%2Fskip%2Fimages%2Fquote-fb.png\" class=\"button fbthis\" title=\"Share on Facebook\" data-url=\"https://www.skipprichard.com/fuel-a-lifelong-love-affair-with-your-customers/\" target=\"_blank\"><i></i>Share on Facebook</a></li></ul></blockquote></div>\n<p>&nbsp;</p>\n<h2>The Growing Demographic</h2>\n<p><strong>What are Transformational Consumers? How is this changing company strategy?</strong></p>\n<p>Transformational Consumers are a massive and growing group of people who see all of life as a series of projects to change their own behavior for the healthier, wealthier and wiser. They know that this behavior change will be hard, but they believe with all their hearts that it’s possible, and they believe that they can change anything about their lives if they can master their own habits and behavior.</p>\n<p>So they are constantly on the lookout for products, services and content they think might help. They are early adopters, and they tend to have great influence on the buying behavior of the people around them.</p>\n<p>I like to joke that if you have ever been vegan <em>and</em> paleo at different times in your life, you’re probably a Transformational Consumer. Most entrepreneurs are Transformational Consumers. The head of product for Airbnb once told me that they see both their hosts <em>and</em> their guests as Transformational Consumers.</p>\n<p>One important takeaway here is that this is not a niche: over 50% of US adult customers we surveyed said that they use digital or real world products several times a week, or more often, in an effort to reach their healthy, wealthy, wise goals.</p>\n<p>The power of this framework is that it offers businesses a lens through which to more powerfully understand the real-world journeys their customers are taking as they aspire to live better lives. And that shows you how to increase customer engagement, brand love, loyalty and repeat business, as well as reach new audiences. Once you understand your real-life customers’ real-world journeys, <em>that</em> surfaces limitless opportunities to innovate new products, features, services and even marketing messages and content that remove resistance points and trigger progress along customers’ paths.</p>\n<p>&nbsp;</p>\n<h2>Rethink Your Customer</h2>\n<p><strong>How do companies go about rethinking their customer?</strong></p>\n<p>Your customers are not just the people who currently buy your product or your current social media followers. I urge companies to shift to the point of view that their customers are all the people out there who are struggling with the high-level, human problems that the company exists to solve.</p>\n<p>Go out into the real world, do customer research, watch how people operate in real life. You can even start this process by just doing some online listening on the blogs and social media sites (not your owned channels) that your audiences frequent online (reddit, etc.).</p>\n<p>Your goal is to understand and, ideally, visually map out your customers’ real-world journeys of going from having the problem you exist to solve to no longer having that problem. You need to know what stages they go through along their journey, what gets them stuck and unstuck, where they go to do research when they need to know or find something and what words and phrases they naturally use as they try to reach their goals.</p>\n<p>&nbsp;</p>\n<p>&nbsp;</p>\n<h2>Remove Resistance</h2>\n<p><strong><a resistance. How do you remove it?</strong></p>\n<p>Think about it: Anytime you try to level-up your life, whether it’s trying to reach a weight loss goal, to work out more, or to start a side business or meditate every day, there’s a force that pops up in all of us that Steven Pressfield and Freud both call Resistance. It’s the same force that creates procrastination, causes us to get distracted or to sabotage ourselves. It’s generally the force that makes it really, really hard to make behavior changes stick.</p>\n<p>In your customers’ journeys toward their healthy, wealthy and wise goals, Resistance includes any sort of quit point, obstacle, friction or common point of failure. These are the things that get people stuck. There are tons of spiritual, emotional, psychological and neurological root causes of Resistance, but suffice it to say that people often know what changes they need to make; they just find it very difficult to actually make them.<br>\nThis creates a major opportunity for companies to win the love of the people they serve by focusing on removing Resistance.</p>\n<p>You can remove Resistance from your customers’ journeys by creating features and products that take friction out of their path, by reducing the difficulty or cost or number of brain cycles they have to go through to create the habits or changes they want, or by inserting progress triggers into their real-world journey.</p>\n<p>For example, at MyFitnessPal, we learned during customer research that one of the biggest obstacles (points of Resistance) that people experience along their journey from living an unhealthy life to living a healthy one is the cost of eating healthy food and the difficulty and time involved in cooking healthfully. So every team in the company explored how they might help remove those Resistance points. When it came to content, for example, we created all sorts of recipes and meal plans for feeding a family healthy, home-cooked food on the same budget we learned people were spending on a fast food family dinner ($20). What are a few lessons that you’ve learned from your work mentoring so many inner city high school students?</strong></p>\n<p>I’ve learned that the impact you can have on students can be even greater than you ever imagined. And it is wonderful. Consider this: so often these youth have absolutely no connection to successful, professional adults. When you mentor, you become their ambassador to a future world of commerce, which is often remote, complex, scary and seemingly unattainable.&nbsp; I have found an intergenerational connection forms particularly when you share your own life trajectory.&nbsp; And not the cocktail-party jargon about Mr. Success either, but more the vulnerable mishaps and the bumps and turns along the way. &nbsp;When you piece together life’s uncertainties with strategies that turn out well, you offer kids a truly relatable journey. It’s as if you have said, subliminally, “If I can, <u>YOU </u>can.”</p>\n<p>When you enter into mentorship, you do so blindly.&nbsp; You enter without a return-on-investment in mind but simply a prospect for potential. This phenomenal exchange can result in an unanticipated “light bulb” moment.&nbsp; What I’ve learned is that you might actually set a ripple in motion. &nbsp;After 1500 student letters I’ve received over the course of 15 years of mentoring, I’m witness to a mentor’s impact that is unforeseen.&nbsp; Students, who look like they are not even listening or engaged, write to proclaim gratitude for an obscure detail you thought went unnoticed. Indeed, some mentees have stayed in touch and are young adults now— in their thirties—who have become entrepreneurs, educators, fashionistas, or preachers. Interestingly, the highest compliment of all is that they, too, are paying it forward. What I’ve learned is that you cannot forecast the genuine awe that occurs when you help another and the crescendo of that ripple effect.</p>\n<p>&nbsp;</p>\n\t\t\t<div class=\"tweetable tweetalign_center\" style=\"width:80%\"><b>“</b><blockquote>\n\t\t\t\t<div class=\"tweet\">\n\t\t\t\t\t<p><br>\n“Every great achiever is inspired by a great mentor.” -Lailah Gifty Akita<br>\n</p>\n\t\t\t\t</div>\t\t\t\t<ul class=\"actions\">\n\t\t\t\t\t<li><a href=\"https://twitter.com/intent/tweet?source=tweetbutton&amp;text=%22Every+great+achiever+is+inspired+by+a+great+mentor.%22+-Lailah+Gifty+Akita+https%3A%2F%2Fwww.skipprichard.com%2Fhow-and-why-you-should-become-a-mentor%2F&amp;via=skipprichard\"><i></i>Tweet This</a></li><li><a href=\"http://www.facebook.com/sharer.php?s=100&amp;p[url]=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.skipprichard.com%2Fhow-and-why-you-should-become-a-mentor%2F&amp;p[title]=Quote+from%3A+How+and+Why+You+Should+Become+a+Mentor&amp;p[summary]=%22Every+great+achiever+is+inspired+by+a+great+mentor.%22+-Lailah+Gifty+Akita&amp;p[images][0]=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.skipprichard.com%2Fwp-content%2Fthemes%2Fskip%2Fimages%2Fquote-fb.png\" class=\"button fbthis\" title=\"Share on Facebook\" data-url=\"https://www.skipprichard.com/how-and-why-you-should-become-a-mentor/\" target=\"_blank\"><i></i>Share on Facebook</a></li></ul></blockquote></div>\n<p>&nbsp;</p>\n<p><strong>What are a few of the benefits of corporate mentoring with students?</strong></p>\n<p>The difficulty with answering this question is keeping it to a “few.” Starting with the mentor, the benefits include the connections and friendships you make. You meet incredible, knowledge-craving students who will cherish every day that you make time for them. Also, you get to know your fellow employees, from senior management to administration, in a wholly new, shared experience of giving back. These positive experiences are brought back to the workplace and ultimately impact the culture.</p>\n<p>Indeed, one of the benefits of offering corporate mentorship is that millennials prefer to work for a company with a robust corporate social responsibility program.&nbsp; Therefore, more qualified employees will seek jobs there. I believe they want to engage in good works activities that they might not be able to find or fund of their own volition. Employees feel a greater sense of loyalty to a company that isn’t solely profit driven but is a “company with a soul.”</p>\n<p>Lastly, the employees find a unique way in which they can serve their company by sharing their professional knowledge as ambassadors in the community. Will those mentees go home and tell their parents about you and the company you work for? You bet. Will the school remember the corporation when it comes time to send their finest students for potential employment? Absolutely. And will an employee find like-minded people within the corporation who will all want to build on the mentoring connection you have? You must understand you before you can possibly understand others and how to influence them.</p>\n<p>If you’re a leader of leaders, you want to understand your team, how they interpret the world, their unique way of leading. A powerful team is made up of a diverse group of leadership styles.</p>\n<p>Lolly Daskal’s new book, <a href=\"https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/1101981350/leaderinsigh-20\" class=\"\" target=\"_blank\"><em>The Leadership Gap: What Gets Between You and Your Greatness</em></a>, introduces her system to help executives discover their own leadership style and how to leverage their strengths. If you want to take your career up a notch, this is for you.</p>\n<p>Lolly is not only a personal friend of mine, but she has racked up numerous awards and accolades ranging from Inc’s Top 50 Leadership and Management Experts to Huffington Post saying she is the most inspiring leader in the world. What are some of the qualities that you notice that makes a leader stand out?</strong></p>\n<p>For over three decades, I have worked as a leadership coach and business consultant around the world, spanning 14 countries and hundreds of companies. Many years ago when I first started, I found an interesting pattern that was showing up within everyone I was working with, even across cultures. Over time I distilled that pattern into seven archetypes, each archetype with its own quality that sets it apart.</p>\n<p>First, there’s the leader I call the <strong>Rebel</strong>, who leads with confidence and wants to make an impact in the world. And Rebels do start revolutions—but not through revolts and uprisings. Rebels are the quiet warriors who embark on quests to achieve remarkable things. They overcome formidable obstacles to save the project, the team, or the company. They ask, “How can I push the envelope?”</p>\n<p>Rebels need confidence to succeed—not the kind of confidence that means standing in front of the mirror and saying, “<em>I’m the best and the brightest</em>,” but knowing your capabilities and competencies, knowing what you are good at, and what skills you have mastered. Confidence is simply knowing what you’re able to do. Tell us more.</strong></p>\n<p>Most successful individuals have a certain set of skills that got them to the top of their game. But there comes a time that those same skill sets stop working, and you have to learn to pivot to keep succeeding. Most of us rely on what we know and expect it to be sustainable, but if we are not changing, evolving and growing, we are not going to remain successful leaders.</p>\n<p>Within the seven archetypes, this principle is expressed as shadows or gaps that exist within each:</p>\n<p>The Rebel who needs to be confident has a gap of feeling like an Imposter, paralyzed by self-doubt. This gap often takes the form of negative internal messages: <em>You are not smart enough, good enough, bright enough to make a big impact. You didn’t go to good schools or get the right education. People are judging you. </em><em>&nbsp;</em></p>\n<p>The Explorer, who is all about using intuition, has a gap of being the Exploiter, who manipulates. Exploration means letting go of control, and those who struggle with turning loose often try to find their way by manipulating and exploiting others.</p>\n<p>The Truth Teller has the gap of becoming the Deceiver, who creates suspicion. This one is easy to spot. It’s the leader who withholds information, the boss who tells half-truths, the manager who doesn’t address concerns. When people don’t know what they need to know, rumors and speculation run wild, creating a culture of suspicion and paranoia.</p>\n<p>The courageous Hero has the gap of becoming the passive Bystander—someone who does and says nothing regardless of what they see or hear. Driven by fear, the Bystander plays small and stays stuck where they are.</p>\n<p>The Inventor, who is all about integrity, has the gap of being the corrupt Destroyer who is focused on doing things cheaper and faster. The Destroyer’s lack of integrity permits quick fixes, cutting corners and compromising quality and standards.</p>\n<p>The Navigator, who focuses on giving and earning trust, has the gap of coming across as the arrogant Fixer. The Fixer tells people what to do instead of navigating with them and is so aggressive that people dismiss them as arrogant by nature. Fixers see the needs of others as more important than their own, and they move from <em>wanting </em>to help to <em>needing </em>to help. They primarily want to be needed.</p>\n<p>Finally, the loyal Knight has the gap of becoming the self-serving Mercenary. Without the understanding that leadership is about serving others, they can’t engender loyalty from those they lead. Fast.</p>\n<p>&nbsp;</p>\n\t\t\t<div class=\"tweetable tweetalign_center\" style=\"width:80%\"><b>“</b><blockquote>\n\t\t\t\t<div class=\"tweet\">\n\t\t\t\t\t<p></p>\n<p>“Never let others define what success means for you.” -Alison Eyring</p>\n<p></p>\n\t\t\t\t</div>\t\t\t\t<ul class=\"actions\">\n\t\t\t\t\t<li><a href=\"https://twitter.com/intent/tweet?source=tweetbutton&amp;text=%22Never+let+others+define+what+success+means+for+you.%22+-Alison+Eyring+https%3A%2F%2Fwww.skipprichard.com%2Fpractice-intelligent-restraint-to-drive-your-growth%2F&amp;via=skipprichard\"><i></i>Tweet This</a></li><li><a href=\"http://www.facebook.com/sharer.php?s=100&amp;p[url]=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.skipprichard.com%2Fpractice-intelligent-restraint-to-drive-your-growth%2F&amp;p[title]=Quote+from%3A+Practice+Intelligent+Restraint+to+Drive+Your+Growth&amp;p[summary]=%22Never+let+others+define+what+success+means+for+you.%22+-Alison+Eyring&amp;p[images][0]=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.skipprichard.com%2Fwp-content%2Fthemes%2Fskip%2Fimages%2Fquote-fb.png\" class=\"button fbthis\" title=\"Share on Facebook\" data-url=\"https://www.skipprichard.com/practice-intelligent-restraint-to-drive-your-growth/\" target=\"_blank\"><i></i>Share on Facebook</a></li></ul></blockquote></div>\n<p>&nbsp;</p>\n<p>That’s why I was drawn to the work of Dr. Alison Eyring. I asked her to share some of these principles with us so we could learn from her research into what she calls “intelligent restraint.” Alison Eyring is the founder and CEO of <a href=\"https://www.organisationsolutions.com/\" target=\"_blank\">Organisation Solutions</a>, and she has advised some of the world’s most innovative companies on leadership and growth.</p>\n<p>&nbsp;</p>\n<h2>Solve Your Growth Challenge</h2>\n<p><strong>How has competing in long-distance runs and triathlons impacted your approach to business?</strong></p>\n<p>Like all business leaders, I struggle to drive my business to perform today, as I also lead transformation for the future – all without damaging the business or my team. It’s so much easier to focus on just one of those things, but we have to do all three for long-term success. &nbsp;My experience training for endurance races led me to discover a growth philosophy I call “Intelligent Restraint” that helps solve this growth challenge.</p>\n<p>&nbsp;</p>\n<p><strong>Can you tell us more about “Intelligent Restraint”?<a href=\"https://www.skipprichard.com/?attachment_id=13141\" rel=\"attachment wp-att-13141\"><img class=\"alignright size-medium wp-image-13141\" src=\"http://www.skipprichard.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/03/Pacing_growth_3d-300x300.png\" alt=\"\" srcset=\"https://www.skipprichard.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/03/Pacing_growth_3d-300x300.png 300w, https://www.skipprichard.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/03/Pacing_growth_3d-150x150.png 150w, https://www.skipprichard.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/03/Pacing_growth_3d-768x768.png 768w, https://www.skipprichard.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/03/Pacing_growth_3d-500x500.png 500w, https://www.skipprichard.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/03/Pacing_growth_3d-200x200.png 200w, https://www.skipprichard.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/03/Pacing_growth_3d-400x400.png 400w, https://www.skipprichard.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/03/Pacing_growth_3d-280x280.png 280w, https://www.skipprichard.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/03/Pacing_growth_3d-120x120.png 120w, https://www.skipprichard.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/03/Pacing_growth_3d-560x560.png 560w, https://www.skipprichard.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/03/Pacing_growth_3d.png 900w\" sizes=\"(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px\"></a></strong></p>\n<p>Intelligent Restraint is a growth mindset that helps you build the right capabilities for growth at the right pace. Sometimes it means going slower, and other times it means going faster.</p>\n<p>When you are training for an endurance race, you have to push yourself to go as far and as fast as you can but then no further so that you don’t get hurt or burned out. &nbsp;In my book, I describe practical ways leaders can apply this growth mindset. For example, you can define and measure “maximum capacity” of the business and then create a plan to bridge the gap between current levels of performance and “maximum capacity.”</p>\n<p>Another way leaders can put this way of thinking to work is by practicing what I call “Rules of Intelligent Restraint.” Like rules of restraint in endurance training, these rules help leaders drive growth in a way that conserves energy and can be sustained. My favorite rule is “routines beat strengths.”</p>\n<p>&nbsp;</p>\n\t\t\t<div class=\"tweetable tweetalign_center\" style=\"width:80%\"><b>“</b><blockquote>\n\t\t\t\t<div class=\"tweet\">\n\t\t\t\t\t<p></p>\n<p>“Routines beat strengths.” -Alison Eyring</p>\n<p></p>\n\t\t\t\t</div>\t\t\t\t<ul class=\"actions\">\n\t\t\t\t\t<li><a href=\"https://twitter.com/intent/tweet?source=tweetbutton&amp;text=%22Routines+beat+strengths.%22+-Alison+Eyring+https%3A%2F%2Fwww.skipprichard.com%2Fpractice-intelligent-restraint-to-drive-your-growth%2F&amp;via=skipprichard\"><i></i>Tweet This</a></li><li><a href=\"http://www.facebook.com/sharer.php?s=100&amp;p[url]=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.skipprichard.com%2Fpractice-intelligent-restraint-to-drive-your-growth%2F&amp;p[title]=Quote+from%3A+Practice+Intelligent+Restraint+to+Drive+Your+Growth&amp;p[summary]=%22Routines+beat+strengths.%22+-Alison+Eyring&amp;p[images][0]=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.skipprichard.com%2Fwp-content%2Fthemes%2Fskip%2Fimages%2Fquote-fb.png\" class=\"button fbthis\" title=\"Share on Facebook\" data-url=\"https://www.skipprichard.com/practice-intelligent-restraint-to-drive-your-growth/\" target=\"_blank\"><i></i>Share on Facebook</a></li></ul></blockquote></div>\n<p>&nbsp;</p>\n<div class=\"callout\" style=\"width:100%;\"><div class=\"calloutinner\" style=\"\">\n\t<h3><strong>Alison's</strong> <strong>8 Insights from Endurance Training</strong></h3>\n<p></p>\n<ol>\n<li><strong>Always train for the right race.</strong></li>\n<li><strong>Don’t let any mountain defeat you.</strong></li>\n<li><strong>Be good enough when good is enough.</strong></li>\n<li><strong>Find many ways to maintain your own energy.</strong></li>\n<li><strong>Don’t spend your life doing only what you do well.</strong></li>\n<li><strong>Never let others define what success means for you.</strong></li>\n<li><strong>Be courageous and be humble; persevere and be willing to stop.</strong></li>\n<li><strong>Never be intimidated by anyone who looks stronger and faster than you.</strong></li>\n</ol>\n<p></p></div></div>\n\n<p>&nbsp;</p>\n<h2><strong>Train for the Right Race</strong></h2>\n<p><strong>How do leaders find the right balance between the sprint and the marathon?</strong></p>\n<p>You can’t sprint and run long distance unless you’ve trained properly. A midfielder in soccer, for example, will sprint the entire game AND also run several miles. They’ve trained for this. On the other hand, if you ask a world class sprinter to run a marathon tomorrow, they might possibly complete a half marathon but they’ll be in tremendous pain.</p>\n<p>As leaders, we need to train our business and our people for the right race. We all want to succeed over the long-term as a business, but there is seldom a long-term unless we can deliver in the short-term and have enough energy to keep going.
